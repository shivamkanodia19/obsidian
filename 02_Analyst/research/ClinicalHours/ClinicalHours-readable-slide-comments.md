# ClinicalHours Investor Pitch - Readable Slide Comments

Extracted from the annotated PDF sticky notes. For deeper research notes, sources, and Claude Design implementation guidance, use `clinicalhours-deck-claude-design-comments-2026-04-21.md`.

## Slide 1 - Cover

- Make this feel like credible healthcare education infrastructure, not a sparse title card.
- Use refined live-site brand: dark hero/product texture, strong ClinicalHours wordmark, visible product/map signal.
- Turn Find / Apply / Track into three small step labels; keep subtitle investor-sharp.

## Slide 2 - Student pain anecdote

- Replace centered text-only treatment with before-state visual: inbox/search collage plus 6 hrs / 12 emails / 2 replies cards.
- Add takeaway: Clinical access is still negotiated manually.
- Label these numbers as pilot/student interview data unless formally sourced.

## Slide 3 - Problem

- Source or reframe the 150,000 pre-med claim; consider anchoring with AAMC 54,699 2025 applicants and larger pre-med funnel.
- Show three fragmented jobs: discovery, application, hour documentation.
- Add source/assumption line and make Zero standard process more visual.

## Slide 4 - Before/after workflow

- Redesign as two workflow lanes: Today vs ClinicalHours.
- Make 6 hrs -> under 5 min the hero delta.
- Use search/apply/track icons, show time saved clearly, and remove stray blue square.

## Slide 5 - Clinic coordinator pain

- Push the buyer pain harder: 1,500+ emails should dominate the slide.
- Add dark inbox/application queue texture and consequences: missed follow-ups, spreadsheet tracking, no visibility.
- Label the 1,500+ number as clinic partner interview/estimate if true.

## Slide 6 - Solution overview

- Turn bullets into a two-sided platform diagram: student workflow left, clinic workflow right, ClinicalHours data layer center.
- Backbone: Find -> Apply -> Track; clinic side: Review -> Schedule -> Communicate -> Manage.
- Use outcome cards and only keep 70% reduction if sourced.

## Slide 7 - Find product

- Make UI larger, crisper, and consistent with other product slides.
- Include search bar, filters, opportunity count, distance/specialty/availability, and possibly map/pin motif.
- Make the 9,000+ / 9,247 / 9,516 count consistent across deck and live site.

## Slide 8 - Apply product

- Clarify reusable standardized application, not just a form screenshot.
- Show 4-step progress rail and Reusable profile badge.
- Keep only a few readable fields; emphasize saved progress and one standard across clinics.

## Slide 9 - Track product

- Connect dashboard directly to AMCAS-ready activity records.
- Show hours, clinics, shifts, reflection notes, supervisor/contact, export module.
- Add AAMC Work and Activities source note and optionally competency mapping chip.

## Slide 10 - Clinic admin

- Make this the buyer ROI slide: a lightweight intake/volunteer ATS.
- Show pipeline stages, filters, interview invite or bulk email action.
- Tie visual to replacing inbox triage, spreadsheets, and follow-up reminders.

## Slide 11 - Traction

- Use exact monthly datapoints, not just a smooth curve.
- Define users: registered, active, waitlist, etc.
- Add one more usage metric if possible: applications, searches, hours logged, saved opportunities, retention.

## Slide 12 - Market size

- Replace decorative TAM/SAM/SOM cards with bottom-up equations and assumptions.
- Use AAMC applicant data as source anchor; clearly mark pre-med funnel assumptions.
- Use sans numerals consistent with the rest of the deck.

## Slide 13 - Competition

- Clean up 2x2 labels and point collisions; put ClinicalHours in a highlighted top-right position.
- Add credible adjacent competitors: Clinician Nexus, CORE ELMS/Exxat, Volgistics/Better Impact, Handshake/VolunteerMatch.
- Explain gap: no pre-med-first discovery + standardized intake + AMCAS tracking.

## Slide 14 - Business model

- Make this look like a real GTM/pricing slide with two large pricing cards.
- Separate Phase 1 student density, Phase 2 clinic revenue, Phase 3 premium conversion.
- Add mini ARR scenario for 5, 50, and 250 clinics.

## Slide 15 - Team

- Replace generic icons with headshots or polished founder cards.
- Show complementary founder-market fit: product/GTM plus domain/full-stack development.
- Use Texas A&M as the launch wedge and tighten bullets to proof points.

## Slide 16 - Ask

- Frame $10K as buying the next proof milestone: 1,000 users and 5 paying clinic partners.
- Use two panels: Use of funds and 6-month proof points.
- Show how each budget line maps to growth, legal/contracts, product, and operations.

## Slide 17 - Closing

- Make closing rhyme with cover: dark refined brand slide plus product strip.
- Add strong closing line: Standardizing how pre-med clinical experience gets found, managed, and documented.
- Include URL, founders/contact, and QR code if available.
