## Claude Design Master Brief

You are redesigning a seed/pre-seed investor pitch deck for ClinicalHours, a two-sided pre-med clinical opportunity platform. The deck should feel like credible healthcare education infrastructure, not a generic student app. Preserve the current narrative order unless a slide comment explicitly says to merge or reframe. Keep all major claims editable and footnoted where possible.

Visual direction: use a refined version of the live ClinicalHours brand. The live site uses dark editorial hero imagery, high-contrast product surfaces, clinical opportunity search, and a national network feel. Bring that energy into the deck, but make it calmer and more investor-readable: deep charcoal/navy backgrounds for hero/problem moments, white or near-white product/data surfaces, bright clinical blue for action, restrained green for successful flow, and red only for pain/status quo. Avoid overusing the American flag motif from the website; it can appear as a subtle cover/closing texture, not as the whole deck identity.

Recommended system:
- Canvas: 16:9, consistent 72 px outside margins, 12-column grid.
- Fonts: modern sans for most text, such as Inter, Satoshi, Aptos, or Manrope. Use a mono only for tiny UI/data labels. Remove the current large serif numerals unless used intentionally and consistently.
- Palette: near-black `#06080D`, off-white `#F7F8FA`, slate `#5B6472`, clinical blue `#2563EB`, softer blue `#DBEAFE`, success green `#14B87A`, alert red `#EF4444`.
- Component style: product screenshots should sit in consistent browser frames with subtle shadow and 8 px radius. Use the same frame style on slides 7-10 and closing.
- Evidence style: every major number should have a small source/assumption line. Unsourced experiential numbers can stay, but should be labeled as "pilot interview" or "founder/customer interview" if true.
- Investor readability: each slide needs one clear takeaway sentence at the top or left, then supporting proof. The current deck has clean whitespace, but too many slides feel underbuilt or unsupported.

## System Understanding To Preserve

ClinicalHours is not just "Find. Apply. Track." The live app structure suggests a broader workflow system:
- Student side: opportunity search, map/list browsing, saved opportunities, applications, hour tracking, AMCAS activity generation, competency mapping, LOR tracker, timeline planner, school list, direct application finder.
- Clinic side: hospital/clinic dashboard, positions, applications, applicant profiles, interviews, email communication, waitlists, volunteer/team management, activity logs, settings.
- Network logic: more opportunity supply improves student discovery; more student demand makes clinics care about standardized intake; standardized intake creates the verified activity record; verified records create premium student utility around AMCAS and competencies.
- VC framing: the deck should convince an investor this is not a nice-to-have directory. It is an emerging workflow standard for a fragmented clinical experience marketplace.

- AMCAS Work and Activities allows up to 15 experiences, up to four occurrences per experience, and up to three "most meaningful" experiences with an extra 1,325 characters. Source: https://students-residents.aamc.org/how-apply-medical-school-amcas/section-5-amcas-application-work-and-activities

- Johns Hopkins pre-professional advising says clinical experience is critical to a pre-health portfolio and that direct patient interaction helps prove comfort in clinical settings. Source: https://studentaffairs.jhu.edu/preprofadvising/pre-medhealth/relevant-experience/shadowing/

Clinician Nexus covers clinical education/student management workflows, CORE ELMS covers clinical education placement/compliance/hours tracking, and Volgistics covers volunteer scheduling/application/hour tracking. These are credible comparables but not pre-med-first student discovery plus application plus AMCAS documentation. Sources: https://cliniciannexus.com/education-suite, https://www.peoplegrove.com/products/core-elms/, https://www.volgistics.com/
