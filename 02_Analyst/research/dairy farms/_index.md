## 📊 Research Areas

### Economics & Resource Usage
- Water consumption by farm size (conventional vs. robotic)
- Energy efficiency (kWh/cow/year with cost analysis)
- Labor models and productivity per worker
- Capital costs and payback timeline
- Profitability by farm size

### Operational Models
- Conventional: 2-3 fixed milking times/day with scheduled labor
- Robotic (AMS): 24/7 voluntary milking with sensor-driven management
- Production flexibility and milk quality implications

### Key Findings
- Robotic farms use 15-20% less water per cow
- Robotic farms use 18-22% less energy per cow
- Robotic farms break even only at 500+ cow scale
- Quality premium (lower SCC) offsets energy cost disadvantage
- Large conventional farms ($19.14/cwt) vs. large robotic ($16.50-$18.00/cwt est.)
