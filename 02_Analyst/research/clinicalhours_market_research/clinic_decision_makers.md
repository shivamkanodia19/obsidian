# Clinic Decision-Makers & Organizational Structure

**Status:** Awaiting research agent findings  
**Last Updated:** 2026-04-16

## Roles & Titles

### Primary Decision-Makers
- **Executive Director / CEO** — Overall strategy, budget approval, final decision
- **Operations Manager / Chief Operations Officer** — Day-to-day operations, volunteer program oversight
- **Volunteer Coordinator / Volunteer Manager** — Manages the volunteer application process, training, scheduling
- **HR Manager / Human Resources** — Hiring process, compliance, background checks

### Secondary Stakeholders
- **Board of Directors** — May require approval for software purchases above certain threshold
- **Clinical Director / Medical Director** — Involvement for HIPAA/compliance decisions
- **Finance Manager / Accounting** — Budget authority, contract negotiation

---

## Organizational Structure Patterns

### Small Free Clinics (10-30 volunteers)
- **Typical structure:** Executive Director + 1-2 volunteer coordinators (part-time or shared role)
- **Decision-making:** ED has final say; VC provides input on operational fit
- **Budget authority:** ED approves purchases under $5K; Board required above
- **Timeline:** Decision in 2-4 weeks

### Medium Clinics (30-100 volunteers)
- **Typical structure:** ED + Operations Manager + Volunteer Coordinator (dedicated)
- **Decision-making:** Operations Manager champions, ED approves
- **Budget authority:** Operations can approve up to $10K; ED approval above
- **Timeline:** Decision in 4-6 weeks

### Large Clinics (100+ volunteers)
- **Typical structure:** ED + COO + Operations Manager + 2+ Volunteer Coordinators
- **Decision-making:** Volunteer Coordinator recommends, Operations Manager approves, ED/Board for major purchases
- **Budget authority:** Operations can approve up to $25K; Board required above
- **Timeline:** Decision in 6-8 weeks

---

## Budget Approval Cycles

- **Fiscal year:** Typically Jan-Dec or July-June (varies by nonprofit)
- **Budget planning:** 2-3 months before fiscal year start
- **Emergency purchases:** Can be approved mid-year if justified
- **Grant cycles:** Many clinics operate on grant funding (annual or biannual)

---

## Key Decision Factors

1. **Operational fit** — Does it solve current pain point?
2. **Cost** — Within budget? Can be justified to ED/Board?
3. **Implementation effort** — How much training/change management?
4. **Vendor credibility** — References from similar clinics?
5. **Technical requirements** — Does it work with existing systems?
6. **Timeline** — How soon needed vs how soon can be implemented?

---

## Cold Email Contact Strategy

**Best approach:** 
- Primary contact: Volunteer Coordinator or Operations Manager (understands pain point)
- Secondary contact: Executive Director (approves budget)
- Reference: "I'd love to learn how [VC Name] is managing your volunteer program currently"

**Why:** VC is most likely to engage with operational tool; ED approval comes later if VC champions it
