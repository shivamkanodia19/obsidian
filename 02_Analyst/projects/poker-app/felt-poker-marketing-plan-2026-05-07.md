# Felt Poker Marketing Plan - TikTok Slideshow First

**Status:** Execution ready  
**Primary channel:** TikTok faceless slideshows  
**Core constraint:** Market only what the product can honestly support  
**Near-term goal:** Build audience + waitlist before pushing harder on product conversion  

---

## What The Vault Says The Product Is

### Current product reality
- Browser-based poker app
- Phase 1 exists as a poker UI / single-player base
- Phase 2 multiplayer is planned, not the current marketing truth

### Product direction
- Real-time multiplayer later: persistent tables, rejoinable seats, chip tracking, 6 players max
- Training mode should be grounded in shared poker-engine logic, not vague AI claims
- Product principle: do not polish or over-market coaching until the underlying logic is reliable

### Marketing implication
- Do **not** lead with "AI poker coach"
- Do **not** lead with multiplayer unless that experience is actually usable
- Lead with **useful poker decision content** and position Felt as the place to practice better decisions

---

## Strategic Positioning

### Best initial angle
Felt Poker should first win attention as a **poker improvement brand** rather than a generic poker app brand.

That fits all 3 facts in the vault:
- single-player foundation exists
- training mode is strategically important
- product credibility depends on logic-backed coaching

### Positioning statement
Felt Poker helps players think through poker spots faster and better with a browser-based experience built around real decision logic, not fake solver cosplay.

### Messaging hierarchy
1. **Top of funnel:** "Become harder to play against."
2. **Middle:** "Study real spots. Learn the logic. Stop punting common decisions."
3. **Bottom:** "Try Felt to practice spots / play through decisions in-browser."

### What to avoid
- "Master poker with AI"
- "Play with friends" if multiplayer is not live
- broad claims like "improve instantly"
- heavy feature selling before there is social proof

## Audience

### Primary audience
- Beginner-to-intermediate poker players
- People who consume poker tips but do not use solvers seriously
- Casual players who want to stop making obvious mistakes in home games or low-stakes online games

### Secondary audience
- Poker TikTok viewers who like quizzes, hand breakdowns, and controversy
- Home-game players who want sharper instincts before multiplayer features arrive

### Not the first audience
- High-stakes solver natives
- Advanced theory grinders expecting deep GTO precision on day 1

---

## Main Growth Thesis

Faceless slideshow TikToks work for poker because poker content naturally creates:
- curiosity
- disagreement
- self-testing
- saves and shares

So the job of content is:
1. stop the scroll with a painful poker mistake or interesting spot
2. make the viewer commit mentally to an action
3. reveal the logic simply
4. turn curiosity into a profile click or waitlist signup

This is not "brand content." It is **poker spot media** with Felt attached.

---

## Content Pillars

### 1. Spot Breakdown Slideshows
Use hand scenarios where viewers choose between fold / call / raise.

Why it works:
- native to slideshow format
- high comment volume
- easy to batch create

Examples:
- "You flop top pair here. Are you betting or checking?"
- "Most low-stakes players torch money in this spot."
- "Would you call this river jam?"

### 2. Common Leaks / Punt Fixes
Show one mistake that weak players repeat.

Examples:
- overvaluing top pair
- calling too wide preflop out of position
- bluffing into under-bluffed pools
- not sizing value bets big enough

### 3. Poker Myths / Unpopular Truths
These are good for reach because they trigger debate.

Examples:
- "Aggressive" is not the same as good
- "Balanced" is overrated at low stakes
- "Most players do not lose because of coolers"

### 4. Quick Training Prompts
Turn content into mini drills.

Examples:
- "Pause on slide 2 and choose your action"
- "Rank these 3 river options"
- "What range are you targeting?"

This pillar is the cleanest bridge into Felt as a product.

### 5. Build-In-Public Proof
Show the product carefully, but only when it helps trust.

Examples:
- "We built this hand trainer because people keep punting this exact spot"
- "Testing a browser tool for poker decision reps"
- "What kind of spot should Felt turn into drills next?"

Keep this under 20% of total posts.

## TikTok Slideshow Format

### Core template
1. Slide 1: strong hook
2. Slide 2: game setup
3. Slide 3: key action point
4. Slide 4: "what are you doing here?"
5. Slide 5: answer
6. Slide 6: short logic
7. Slide 7: common mistake
8. Slide 8: CTA

### Hook formulas
- "Most 1/2 players lose money here."
- "If you always c-bet this flop, you're probably punting."
- "90% of casual players choose the wrong option here."
- "This river call feels strong. It's usually a trap."
- "One decision like this can wipe your whole session."

### CTA formulas
- "Follow for daily poker spots."
- "Comment your action before the reveal."
- "Save this for your next session."
- "If you want drills like this in-browser, join the waitlist."

### Visual rules
- very little text per slide
- one decision per post
- clean color contrast, poker-table visual cues
- no tiny solver charts
- every slide should be understandable in under 1 second

## Funnel Design

### Phase A: before product is ready for broad activation
TikTok slideshow -> profile -> waitlist / email capture -> early-access list -> user interviews / testers

### Phase B: when decision-drill or training experience is usable
TikTok slideshow -> profile -> free drill / web demo -> email capture -> repeat usage

### Phase C: when multiplayer is actually live
TikTok slideshow -> profile -> play now / invite friends -> table creation / join code flow

### Best CTA right now
If the product is not yet strong enough for mass conversion, optimize for:
- follows
- comments
- waitlist signups
- playtester applications

Do not force hard product CTAs too early.

## 30-Day TikTok Plan

### Cadence
- 2 posts/day
- 5 days/week
- 1 batch-production session weekly

### Weekly mix
- 4 spot breakdowns
- 2 leak-fix posts
- 2 myth / hot-take posts
- 1 training prompt post
- 1 light build-in-public post

### Week 1: Attention
- common low-stakes mistakes
- simple preflop and flop spots
- hooks optimized for comments

### Week 2: Pattern Recognition
- repeat formats that got saves
- introduce turn and river decisions
- start teasing Felt as a place to practice spots

### Week 3: Identity
- create recurring series names
- examples: "Punt Check," "River Test," "Would You Fold?"
- begin stronger profile CTA

### Week 4: Conversion Test
- best-performing hooks reused with better CTA
- 2-3 posts directly tied to trying Felt / joining waitlist
- ask viewers what formats or spots they want next

## Creative Operating System

### Production workflow
1. Write 20 hooks from common poker mistakes
2. Turn each into an 8-slide template
3. Batch design 10-15 posts at once
4. Post, log results, cut losers fast
5. Double down on any format that gets saves + comments

### Reuse system
- one good hook can generate 5-10 scenarios
- one scenario can become TikTok, IG carousel, X thread, Reddit post
- comments become the next batch of content ideas

### 70-20-10 split
- 70% proven slideshow formats
- 20% improved variants
- 10% experiments: humor, build-in-public, controversy

## Brand Voice

Felt should sound:
- sharp
- calm
- credible
- slightly challenging

It should not sound:
- hypey
- bro-guru
- fake-solver elitist
- overly technical for casual players

Good tone:
- "Most players lose money here because they overrate hand strength."
- "You do not need solver brain to fix this leak."

Bad tone:
- "Our AI revolutionizes poker improvement."
