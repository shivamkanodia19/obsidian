# Felt TikTok Slideshow Retention Strategy - 2026-05-15

**Goal:** adapt the broader Felt attraction/retention thesis into a 5-slide TikTok slideshow that feels psychologically sticky without drifting into casino tactics, fake urgency, or compliance risk.

**Reference note used:** `02_Analyst/projects/felt/strategy/felt-attraction-retention-strategy-2026-05-14.md`

## Findings First

The slideshow gets stronger when it borrows the **behavioral structure** of sticky products, not the cheap language of gambling ads.

What actually maps from the main retention note:

- `fast activation` beats feature dumping
- `visible state` beats abstract promises
- `mastery loops` beat empty rewards
- `social return surfaces` beat solo compulsion loops
- `relevance and control` beat manipulative mystery

What does **not** map well:

- fake jackpot energy
- “win big” framing
- countdown pressure with no real product reason
- loud casino color abuse
- screenshots that look chaotic or untrustworthy

## Core Thesis

For Felt, “addictive” should mean:

- the first slide creates a clean curiosity gap
- the second slide resolves that tension fast
- the middle slides prove that the product is organized and rewarding to use
- the final slide makes returning feel natural, social, and credible

The slideshow should make the viewer think:

- `this solves a real mess`
- `I understand the product quickly`
- `this feels cleaner than the usual way people run games`
- `I can see myself using this with my group`

## Research Conclusions Actually Applied

### 1. The story has to read instantly

TikTok’s official image/carousel guidance emphasizes quick storytelling and clarity. That means each slide needs:

- one dominant message
- one dominant proof crop
- one obvious emotional job

Applied to Felt:

- no more than one major promise per slide
- headlines must read in under 2 seconds
- support crops should explain, not decorate

### 2. Key elements need to stay centered and safe

TikTok’s official guidance also recommends keeping key elements centered so they do not get cropped by the app UI. For this project, that means:

- all essential text stays inside a conservative safe area
- CTA and headline blocks cannot drift into bottom UI danger zones
- screenshots can bleed, but meaning cannot

### 3. Carousel momentum comes from tension and release

The most effective sequence is not five equal slides. It is:

1. `tension`
2. `relief`
3. `proof`
4. `aspiration`
5. `return`

That pacing creates swipe momentum because each frame changes the viewer’s question:

- slide 1: what is the mess?
- slide 2: how does this simplify it?
- slide 3: is it actually organized?
- slide 4: does it make me better?
- slide 5: why would I come back?

### 4. Premium dark mode works when contrast is disciplined

The goal is not “more color” in a generic sense. It is **controlled contrast**:

- warm gold acts as the attention pin
- felt green carries product competence and continuity
- cream UI surfaces provide truth and instant legibility
- black/espresso background creates premium restraint and isolation

The deck works best when gold is rare and deliberate. If everything glows, nothing feels important.

## Slide Sequence Framework

### Slide 1: Tension Hook

Job:

- name the mess
- create instant self-recognition
- make the swipe feel necessary

Best kind of line:

- `Home games need a home.`
- simple, sharp, structural

Avoid:

- adjectives with no proof
- generic “better poker” claims

### Slide 2: Fast Activation

Job:

- resolve tension immediately
- show the setup is light, not heavy

Best kind of proof:

- code/join/host UI
- one-screen understanding

### Slide 3: Visible State

Job:

- prove that the product is legible under real use
- show the table is not chaotic

Best kind of proof:

- stacks
- blinds
- turn state
- room visibility

### Slide 4: Mastery Loop

Job:

- show Felt is not just logistics
- show it helps users sharpen decisions

Best kind of proof:

- trainer UI
- live feedback UI
- coaching/explanation surfaces

### Slide 5: Return Surface

Job:

- make the product feel like a place to come back to
- close with invitation, not sales pressure

Best kind of proof:

- private table continuity
- clean re-entry
- decision practice adjacency

## Copy Rules

- Headline should carry the slide. Do not rely on subtext to explain the idea.
- Keep headline to roughly `4-7` words total when possible.
- Favor short sentence fragments over clever long copy.
- Use plain language: `home`, `code`, `room`, `crew`, `train`, `return`.
- Never use casino-coded phrasing like `jackpot`, `hot streak`, `bet big`, `double down`, or `cash out`.
- CTA tone should feel like product confidence, not performance-marketing desperation.

## Visual Rules

- One hero screenshot per slide.
- Maximum one or two support proof crops.
- The screenshot must show the product state named in the copy.
- Text cannot sit on top of busy UI.
- Empty space is useful only when it increases focus; otherwise it is dead space.
- Reusing the same screenshot is acceptable only if the crop tells a different story.
- Every slide should feel like the same system, but not the same frame repeated five times.

## Color Psychology Rationale

Use a restrained split:

- `80%` dark neutrals for focus and premium tone
- `15%` felt green for product continuity and calm control
- `5%` warm gold for interruption, labels, and CTA emphasis

Interpretation:

- `gold` gets the first glance
- `green` keeps the eye inside the product
- `cream` makes the screenshots feel real and trustworthy
- `black` makes the UI feel elevated rather than noisy

## Anti-Patterns To Avoid

- repeating the same crop without a new message
- letting screenshots become tiny decorative stickers
- oversized copy that collides with proof
- vague claims like `play smarter` with no visible UI support
- red error states, signed-out states, or broken flows
- making Felt look like a cash gambling product

## Practical Creative Brief For Future Iterations

If another agent has to make the next version, the brief should be:

`Build a 5-slide TikTok sequence that uses real felt.bet UI to move from friction -> simplicity -> control -> mastery -> return. Keep all essential text in safe zones. Use one hero proof asset per slide, limited gold accents, and proof crops that are legible in under 2 seconds. Do not use casino language or broken product states.`
