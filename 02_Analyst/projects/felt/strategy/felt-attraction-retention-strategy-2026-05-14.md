# Felt Attraction and Retention Strategy - 2026-05-14

**Status:** deep strategy note  
**Goal:** identify the highest-leverage ways to attract users, make them return, and build a durable product habit without turning Felt into a trashy casino trap

## Brutal Thesis

If you chase "addictive" the dumb way, you will build a worse product, attract lower-quality users, and increase policy, trust, and brand risk.

If you chase it the smart way, Felt should become:

- easy to start
- fast to understand
- satisfying to improve at
- social enough to come back to
- personal enough to feel "mine"

The best consumer products do not win only because they hijack attention. They win because they combine:

- fast activation
- strong personalization
- visible progress
- social reinforcement
- identity/status
- reactivation when users drift away

That is the real playbook.

## What The Best Apps Actually Do

### 1. They personalize the experience fast

TikTok's strongest product advantage is not just short video. It is the speed of cold-start personalization.

- TikTok asks new users to select interests, then refines recommendations from early likes, comments, replays, follows, and skips.
- TikTok weights strong signals more heavily than weak ones.
- TikTok also mixes in some diversity so the feed does not become too narrow.

What this means for Felt:

- do not show the same home screen to everyone
- ask for a primary game and primary intent early
- adapt the home screen quickly based on behavior
- mix familiar actions with one exploratory action

### 2. They optimize each surface for a different job

YouTube explicitly says different product surfaces use different recommendation signals.

- homepage is heavily shaped by watch history
- "Up Next" is shaped mainly by the current video context

What this means for Felt:

- `Home` should optimize for return and routing
- `Poker` should optimize for depth, skill, and social play
- `Blackjack` should optimize for fast repeat sessions
- training surfaces should optimize for improvement, not raw time spent

Do not use one blunt engagement layer across the whole app.

### 3. They turn progress into something visible

Duolingo and Google Maps both make progress legible.

Duolingo:

- treats streaks as a major return mechanism
- improves streak presentation because small changes in felt satisfaction can lift retention
- uses social streaks to turn self-discipline into shared commitment
- uses flexibility tools so the system motivates without becoming too brittle

Google Maps Local Guides:

- gives users points, levels, badges, and perks for contribution
- makes status public and cumulative

What this means for Felt:

- users should always know if they are improving
- progression should be tied to meaningful actions, not empty grind
- status should reward skill, contribution, and reliability, not just volume

### 4. They make social behavior do retention work

Discord is blunt about this because it sits on top of game communities.

- Discord says social play drives longer play and better retention
- official communities create a real home for players between sessions
- events and patch discussion keep people engaged even when they are not actively playing

Reddit is useful for a different reason:

- it is shifting emphasis from passive member count to active participation
- visitors and contributions matter more than vanity membership

What this means for Felt:

- friend play is not a feature add-on; it is a retention engine
- community should be measured by active play, invites, and discussion, not vanity downloads
- the product should create reasons to return with other people, not just alone

### 5. They create identity loops, not just session loops

Spotify Wrapped is a great example.

- it turns usage history into a personal story
- it makes the story shareable
- it connects users, creators, and social sharing in one moment

What this means for Felt:

- users should occasionally get a compelling summary of what kind of player they are
- the app should generate shareable identity moments
- those moments should feel insightful, not spammy

### 6. They use feedback controls to improve relevance

Meta has moved toward more explicit feedback and more visible control over ranking.

- "Interested" and "Not Interested" signals improve relevance
- visible controls reduce the sense that the product is manipulating users invisibly

What this means for Felt:

- let users steer what they see more of
- let them mute surfaces they do not care about
- let them choose whether they want speed, skill, social, or challenge-heavy experiences

Relevance beats generic engagement every time.

## What Maps Well To Felt

### High-Leverage Systems To Build

#### 1. Fast activation and first-session personalization

This is the highest-leverage early system.

New user flow should ask:

- `What do you want to start with?`
  - Poker
  - Blackjack
- `What are you here to do first?`
  - Play
  - Train
  - Play with Friends

Then the app should immediately route to a short, successful first action:

- poker training spot
- quick blackjack hand with feedback
- easy friend invite / join flow

The first 3 minutes matter more than your tenth cosmetic feature.

#### 2. A personalized home screen that gives one obvious next action

The home screen should behave like a smart return surface, not a dashboard graveyard.

Best home cards:

- continue last session
- next recommended training rep
- friend invite / table rejoin
- daily challenge
- weekly progress summary
- one cross-game suggestion

The user should never ask, "Why am I opening this app?"

#### 3. Skill-based progression, not fake casino progression

Felt should not lean on bigger fake bankroll numbers as the main loop.

Better progression:

- poker decision accuracy
- blackjack basic-strategy accuracy
- training streak
- solved spots
- friend-host reputation
- weekly consistency badges
- table-night participation

Users should feel that they are getting better, not just accumulating junk points.

#### 4. Social commitment loops

The best retention loop for Felt is not "open app every day because number go up."

It is:

- my friend is waiting
- our streak is on the line
- our home game is tonight
- I need to finish my side of the challenge

Best Felt versions:

- shared weekly play streak with a friend
- recurring home-game streak
- rivalry cards
- nudge system for inactive table members
- club-based weekly ladder

#### 5. Smart reactivation instead of generic win-back spam

Duolingo tracks resurrected users separately for a reason: returning users are not the same as new users.

Felt should build separate comeback flows:

- lapsed poker player
- lapsed blackjack player
- lapsed trainer
- lapsed friend-group player

Reactivation messages should be specific:

- your table wants to run it back
- 3 quick blackjack spots to sharpen up
- your poker accuracy dipped last session; fix it in 2 minutes
- your weekly recap is ready

#### 6. Shareable identity and recap moments

Good recap moments:

- weekly "What kind of player are you?" summary
- monthly coach report
- best fold / best recovery / biggest leak
- home-game leaderboard summary
- season-end Felt recap

The right recap system creates:

- retention
- word of mouth
- social proof
- content fodder

#### 7. Official community layer

Felt should eventually have one official Discord community.

Why:

- it gives players a place to exist between sessions
- it makes friend-play more durable
- it gives direct feedback and patch discussion
- it creates launch energy and live event opportunities

## What Does Not Map Well To Felt

These are the wrong lessons to copy.

### 1. Endless-feed addiction mechanics

Felt is not TikTok.

Do not force:

- infinite-scroll engagement as the core loop
- content sludge just to fill a home feed
- constant novelty with no skill payoff

TikTok's lesson is personalization speed, not "become a slot machine feed."

### 2. Pure variable-reward casino psychology

Because Felt is already card-game adjacent, leaning too hard into casino cues is dangerous.

Avoid:

- fake urgency
- jackpot-style visual language
- manipulative near-miss effects
- overuse of streak punishment
- meaningless loot-box style reward systems

This may increase short-term compulsion while damaging trust, positioning, and policy safety.

### 3. Vanity metrics as strategy

Downloads, total accounts, and Discord members are not the real story.

Felt should care more about:

- weekly active players
- repeat table creation
- friend invite acceptance
- training completion
- cross-over from poker to blackjack

## Highest-Leverage Product Moves For Felt

### Tier 1: Build First

- personalized onboarding by game and intent
- game-first home screen with one strong next action
- poker-first shared shell and design system
- training streaks and skill-progress feedback
- easy friend invites and rejoin flows
- event-triggered notifications, not generic spam

### Tier 2: Build Next

- shared friend streaks / home-game streaks
- weekly recap and identity cards
- reactivation flows by user type
- lightweight status layers: badges, tiers, host reliability
- social challenge systems

### Tier 3: Build Later

- official Discord integration and events
- clubs / leagues / seasons
- richer recap systems
- creator/UGC loops around hand debates and coaching clips
- deeper social reputation layers

## Felt-Specific Strategy Recommendations

### A. Make poker the main habit engine

Poker should be the deeper identity layer because it has:

- better debate content
- stronger friend-play gravity
- higher skill ceiling
- more interesting coaching moments

Blackjack should be the faster re-entry and lower-friction lane.

That means:

- let poker shape the shell and brand
- use blackjack as a speed lane and habit support lane
- cross-promote blackjack as quick reps when users do not have time for poker

### B. Build a two-speed habit system

Felt should support both:

- deep sessions
- tiny return sessions

Deep session examples:

- poker AI session
- friend table night
- long training review

Tiny session examples:

- one blackjack spot
- one poker decision drill
- check streak and complete one rep

Durable habit products support both full engagement and low-energy maintenance.

### C. Reward contribution, not just consumption

For Felt, contribution can mean:

- hosting a table
- inviting reliable players
- completing training
- reviewing hands
- participating in community events
- sharing useful strategy content

Users who contribute should feel seen.

### D. Measure active participation over passive possession

Good metrics:

- weekly active players
- games started per active user
- training reps per active user
- friend invites sent and accepted
- percent of users who return within 7 days
- percent of users with a streak
- percent of users with a friend-linked loop
- percent of users who cross from poker to blackjack

Bad leading metrics:

- total signups
- total chips
- raw session count with no quality measure

### E. Design notifications like promises, not bait

Good notifications:

- your table is starting
- your friend needs one more game to keep the streak alive
- your 2-minute training rep is ready
- your weekly recap is live

Bad notifications:

- come win big
- huge rewards waiting
- only a few hours left
- you are missing out

## Ethical Guardrails

This category is risky because the product sits near gambling psychology.

Felt should stay on the right side of the line:

- optimize for skill, social play, and repeat value
- keep chips clearly play-money only
- avoid dark patterns and coercive urgency
- avoid making loss or shame the main motivator
- do not make the product feel like a disguised casino funnel

The safest long-term brand is one that feels smart, fun, and social, not predatory.

## Phased Roadmap

### Now

- build personalized onboarding
- unify the shell around poker-first UX
- make home a strong return surface
- ship streaks tied to learning or social play
- improve invites and rejoin flows

### Next

- add recap systems
- add social streaks and weekly ladders
- add segmented reactivation
- add lightweight status and contribution signals

### Later

- clubs / leagues / official community programming
- richer UGC loops
- seasonal identity moments
- deeper player segmentation and recommendations

## End Goal

The end state is not "people cannot stop opening the app."

The end state is:

- users know what Felt is immediately
- poker gives the app depth and identity
- blackjack gives the app quick-entry frequency
- training makes the app feel useful
- friends make the app feel alive
- progression makes the app feel earned
- recaps and identity make the app memorable

That is the high-quality version of habit formation for Felt.
