# Felt Marketing Plan - 2026-05-14

**Status:** Strategic go-to-market plan  
**Product:** `Felt`  
**Market truth:** play-money poker plus blackjack, one account, separate game lanes, web plus mobile view priority

## Brutal Starting Point

Your bottleneck is **not** that Bloom costs money.

Your bottleneck is:

- finding a positioning angle people actually care about
- producing enough truthful creative volume
- keeping the app far away from scammy gambling-ad vibes

Paid AI ad tools are not the missing ingredient. A repeatable content and positioning system is.

## Core Marketing Decision

**Market Felt as a poker-first brand with blackjack as the second lane inside the same app.**

Why:

- poker has better debate content
- poker has stronger friend-play identity
- poker training content is more differentiated
- blackjack is easier to understand but easier to commoditize

If you try to market both games equally from day one, the brand gets fuzzier.

### Launch Weighting

Use this split for the first 6 to 8 weeks:

- `70% poker content`
- `30% blackjack content`

Rebalance only if blackjack meaningfully outperforms poker over several weeks.

## Positioning

### Best Product Position

Felt is a **play-money card app for sharper decisions and better social sessions**.

### Best Brand Promise

`Play cards sharper.`

### Best Public Angle

- poker: improve your decisions, test yourself, play private tables
- blackjack: play fast solo sessions, learn the right move, play private tables with friends

### What To Avoid

- "casino app"
- "AI dealer"
- "win big"
- "make money"
- "cash out"
- "beat the casino"
- "train to gamble"

## Compliance and Platform Reality

### Non-Negotiable Positioning Rules

- market Felt as `18+`
- say `play-money only`
- say `no real-money gambling`
- say `no cash prizes`
- say virtual chips or currency have no real-world value
- avoid minors, college-party vibes, payout screenshots, or jackpot language

### Practical Platform Constraints

#### TikTok

TikTok's current policy treats social casino games as gambling-like activity and says gambling ads may not feature or appeal to young people, must be age-restricted, and may be limited in some formats.  
Source: [TikTok Gambling and Games policy](https://ads.tiktok.com/help/article/tiktok-ads-policy-gambling-and-games?lang=en)

#### Apple Ads

Apple allows simulated gambling ads only on certain placements. Its policy says simulated gambling content may run only on certain placements and may be prohibited or limited in some countries or regions.  
Source: [Apple Ads Policies](https://ads.apple.com/policies)

#### Apple App Review / App Store

Apple's review guidelines still treat gambling as highly regulated, and Apple age-rating guidance explicitly defines simulated gambling and can push these apps into higher ratings depending on frequency/intensity.  
Sources: [App Review Guidelines](https://developer.apple.com/app-store/review/guidelines/), [Apple age ratings definitions](https://developer.apple.com/help/app-store-connect/reference/app-information/age-ratings-values-and-definitions)

#### Google Ads

Google Ads allows social casino advertising only in approved countries and only with social casino certification. It defines social casino games as simulated gambling-style games with no opportunity to win something of value.  
Source: [Google Ads Gambling and games policy](https://support.google.com/adspolicy/answer/15132179?hl=en)

#### Google Play

Google Play is restrictive around real-money gambling and around gambling ads inside Play-distributed apps. It also flags simulated gambling in its policies and rating systems.  
Sources: [Google Play Real-Money Gambling, Games, and Contests](https://support.google.com/googleplay/android-developer/answer/9877032), [Google Play ratings](https://support.google.com/googleplay/answer/6209544?hl=en-GB)

### Brutal Interpretation

Paid growth is possible later, but this is a **restricted and higher-friction category**.

So:

- do not start with paid ads as your main growth plan
- do not build copy that needs policy-exception gymnastics to survive
- do not blur play-money with anything that smells like sweepstakes or real-money play

## Best Channel Strategy

### Channel 1: Organic short-form first

Primary channels:

- TikTok
- Instagram Reels
- YouTube Shorts

Why this is the right first move:

- cheapest volume
- best fit for poker and blackjack decision content
- strongest learning loop on hooks and messaging
- least dependence on restricted paid approval flows

### Channel 2: Landing pages and store capture

Use:

- `felt.bet/`
- `felt.bet/poker`
- `felt.bet/blackjack`

If/when mobile listings matter:

- use one app listing
- use separate creative angles for poker and blackjack traffic
- later use Apple custom product pages and Google Play custom store listings to route poker-intent traffic and blackjack-intent traffic to different store experiences

Sources:

- [Apple Custom Product Pages](https://developer.apple.com/app-store/custom-product-pages/?cid=developer80)
- [Google Play custom store listings](https://support.google.com/googleplay/android-developer/answer/9867158?hl=en-EN)

### Channel 3: Community

Use community as a trust and testing layer, not as a spam channel.

Best places:

- Discord
- adult home-game circles
- poker and blackjack communities where product feedback is welcome
- your own tester list and private groups

Bad idea:

- showing up in communities just to drop installs with zero useful content

### Channel 4: Paid later, not first

Paid acquisition should come **after** organic signal and retention proof.

Best later paid channels:

- Apple Search Ads for high-intent search capture
- selective Meta retargeting if policy approval and creative quality are there
- Google Ads only if certification and category handling are clean

## Content Strategy

### Master Rule

Do not make generic "app promo" content.

Make **decision content** and let Felt be the place where viewers can keep playing.

### Primary Content Pillars

#### Poker Decision Content

Use for reach and authority.

Formats:

- "Would you call here?"
- "Most low-stakes players torch money in this spot."
- "Top pair is not a marriage."
- "The river fold most players miss."

#### Blackjack Decision Content

Use for faster comprehension and repeatable volume.

Formats:

- "Hit, stand, double, or split?"
- "Most beginners misplay this hand."
- "Soft 18 is where people start guessing."
- "You only get this right if you actually know basic strategy."

#### Friend Table Content

Use for social proof and product texture.

Formats:

- private poker table moments
- blackjack friend-table reactions
- invite/rejoin flow demos
- "this is what Felt looks like with friends"

#### Build-In-Public Content

Use sparingly.

Formats:

- "We changed this training feedback because players kept punting the same spot."
- "Poker players wanted X. Blackjack players wanted Y."

Keep this under 20% of total content.

## Account Strategy

### Start With One Main Brand Account

Use one main `Felt` account first.

Why:

- lower operational overhead
- shared brand compounds faster
- early-stage volume is easier to maintain

### How To Prevent Audience Confusion

Use recurring series labels:

- `Poker Spot`
- `Blackjack Move`
- `Friend Table`
- `Felt Build`

Make the game obvious in the first second of every post.

### When To Split Accounts

Only split later if:

- one game is consistently 3x the other in reach and engagement
- viewers start complaining about mixed content
- you can actually maintain two accounts with quality

## Content Production System

### Weekly Production Workflow

1. Record raw footage
2. Pull 20 to 30 usable moments
3. Cut them into templates
4. Publish daily
5. Review hooks and comments
6. Rebuild next week around winners

### What To Record Every Week

- 10 poker decision spots
- 10 blackjack decision spots
- 5 friend-table clips
- 5 product/UI clips

### Posting Cadence

Start with:

- `2 posts/day`
- `5 to 6 days/week`

Suggested mix per week:

- 6 poker posts
- 3 blackjack posts
- 1 friend-mode or build post

## 30-Day Execution Plan

### Week 1

- finalize positioning
- build 5 reusable editing templates
- set up landing pages for poker and blackjack
- record first 30 raw clips

### Week 2

- publish 10 to 12 posts
- test poker hooks vs blackjack hooks
- track which game earns more comments, saves, and clicks

### Week 3

- double down on winning format
- add stronger product CTAs
- record friend-mode proof clips

### Week 4

- publish best-performing variants again
- sharpen landing page/store messaging
- decide whether to test small paid search on Apple or keep leaning organic

## Metrics That Actually Matter

### Content Metrics

- 3-second hold
- completion rate
- shares
- saves
- profile click rate
- click-through to site or install

### Product Metrics

- onboarding completion
- D1 retention by game
- D7 retention by game
- training-session completion
- friend invite rate
- cross-game adoption rate

### Go / No-Go Rule For Paid Spend

Do not scale paid acquisition until:

- one organic format is clearly working
- onboarding is stable
- D1 retention is healthy
- D7 retention is not embarrassing

If retention is weak, paid acquisition is just a faster way to waste money.

## Final Marketing Call

The smartest version of Felt marketing is:

- poker-first public wedge
- blackjack as a clean second lane
- organic short-form first
- content that teaches or tests, not fake ad creative
- one brand account at first
- zero ambiguity about play-money and age gating

That is the version that gives you a chance to grow without looking cheap, deceptive, or legally sloppy.
