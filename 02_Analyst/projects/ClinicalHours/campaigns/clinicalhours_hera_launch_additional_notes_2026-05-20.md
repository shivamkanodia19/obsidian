ClinicalHours launch video brief

Create a polished, high-end product launch video for ClinicalHours, a platform that helps students find clinical opportunities more easily and keep their search, saved clinics, and progress in one place.

This video should feel like a premium startup launch film, but grounded in real product proof. The tone should be calm, credible, modern, and useful. It should not feel overhyped, noisy, or generic. It should feel like a product people can trust right away.

Primary audience:
- pre-med and health-track students looking for clinical hours
- students who are overwhelmed by scattered clinic searches, school documents, screenshots, and random clinic pages

Core message:
Finding clinical hours still feels manual and fragmented. ClinicalHours makes the process feel clearer by putting opportunities on one map, helping students preview clinics, and giving them a cleaner way to save and track what matters.

Important truth boundary:
- do not imply guaranteed placements, guaranteed admissions, guaranteed acceptance, or guaranteed outcomes
- do not invent fake hospitals, fake partnerships, fake reviews, fake user counts beyond what is explicitly shown, fake dashboards, fake patient data, or fake compliance claims
- if product UI appears, it should feel consistent with a real modern healthcare-tech product
- product proof matters more than spectacle

Recommended video length:
- 25 to 35 seconds

Recommended visual direction:
- bright editorial healthcare-tech look
- off-white or warm cream base
- dark navy or near-black typography
- soft blue, mint, peach, and pale gold accents
- rounded cards, clean framing, spacious composition
- crisp, premium typography with strong hierarchy
- product screenshots or screenshot-like framed product scenes should be the hero proof

Recommended motion direction:
- smooth, intentional, premium motion
- clean slide-ins, layered card reveals, masked pans, gentle zooms, soft parallax
- elegant transitions between scenes
- subtle cursor or UI emphasis only when useful
- avoid chaotic motion, glitch effects, hard tech-cyber aesthetics, or overly aggressive speed ramps

Story structure:

1. Problem recognition
Open with the pain immediately. Make the viewer feel that finding clinical hours still feels messy, scattered, and harder than it should be.

Suggested copy direction:
- Still looking for clinical hours?
- The search still feels scattered.
- Tabs, school docs, screenshots, and random clinic pages are not a real system.

2. Map-based payoff
Move quickly into the product payoff. Show that ClinicalHours gives students one clearer place to start.

Suggested copy direction:
- 9,512 openings. One map.
- One map beats ten tabs.
- Start with a clearer search.

3. Clinic preview and filtering
Show that students can see useful clinic information before wasting time on the wrong lead. Make the product feel practical, not abstract.

Suggested copy direction:
- See the clinic before you click.
- Spot fit faster.
- Preview the opportunity before chasing it.

4. Save and track
Show the product becoming more useful after discovery. The viewer should understand that ClinicalHours is not just a directory; it helps carry the workflow forward.

Suggested copy direction:
- Search, save, track, log.
- Keep your search and progress together.
- Save the clinics that matter and track what comes next.

5. Calm CTA
End with a low-friction close. The CTA should feel easy and believable, not pushy.

Suggested copy direction:
- Browse first. Sign in later.
- Start at clinicalhours.org
- Explore opportunities, then save and track when you are ready.

Editing priorities:
- each scene should communicate one clear point
- keep text short and highly readable
- let the product visuals do most of the persuasion
- build from problem to clarity to confidence
- every new scene should feel more useful and more specific than the last one

What to emphasize visually:
- opportunity map
- featured clinic or opportunity card
- account or dashboard state that implies progress and organization
- search, save, journal, or tracking workflows
- clean CTA moment with browse-first energy

What to avoid:
- generic hospital stock footage dominating the video
- fake patient scenes
- cliché inspiration visuals that overpower the product
- dense walls of copy
- overly dark enterprise aesthetics
- loud founder-bro launch energy
- crypto-style motion or flashy neon effects
- anything that feels more like a trailer than a real product launch

Music and pacing:
- modern, light, confident soundtrack
- steady build, not cinematic drama
- pacing should feel premium and fast, but never frantic
- the first 3 seconds must land the pain or payoff clearly

Brand feel:
ClinicalHours should feel like:
- helpful
- focused
- trustworthy
- student-friendly
- operationally clear
- contemporary without being trendy for trend's sake

If you use on-screen product callouts, keep them minimal and useful:
- map
- clinic
- opportunities
- save
- track
- hours
- browse

Do not use language like:
- revolutionize healthcare
- guarantee your future
- never struggle again
- the ultimate platform
- game-changing ecosystem
- AI-powered admissions success

Target end result:
The final video should make a student think:
"This finally feels like a cleaner way to find clinical hours."

If a template is used, adapt the template to this product story rather than forcing ClinicalHours into a flashy or generic launch format. Keep the result product-led, proof-first, and elegant.
