# ClinicalHours Clinic Outreach Strategy Framework

## Strategy Overview: Problem-First Personalization

**Core Principle:** Position ClinicalHours as an operational solution, not a product pitch. Adapted from Summer 2026 internship cold email framework.

**Why this works for clinics:**
- Clinic directors/founders make purchasing decisions based on **immediate operational pain**, not features
- They have limited budgets, so ROI must be obvious (time saved = staff cost savings)
- Volunteer dependency is critical (clinics can't operate without volunteers)
- Manual processes are entrenched (spreadsheets, email, phone-based coordination)

---

## The Internship Strategy → Clinic Adaptation

### Internship Framework (What Worked)

**"Technical Operations Hire" positioning for non-technical founders:**
1. **Show specific understanding** of their operational problem (flight ops dashboard, document extraction, case management)
2. **Propose concrete Week 1 deliverable** (not vague "I want to learn")
3. **Reference proof** (ClinicalHours itself — you built exactly what they need)
4. **Position as builder, not learner** (capability > credentials)

**Result:** 3 companies ready to call (Aerolane, Pace, Work Shield), higher response rate than generic role applications

---

### Clinic Adaptation (Same Logic)

**"Volunteer Operations Expert" positioning for clinic directors:**
1. **Show specific understanding** of their volunteer coordination problem
   - Problem recognition: "140+ volunteers on spreadsheets = 20+ hours/month admin"
   - Pain signal: Retention issues, scheduling chaos, interview follow-ups falling through
   - Mission-specific angle: "Pregnancy resource centers need consistent peer counselors" OR "Emergency clinics need reliable 24/7 coverage"

2. **Propose concrete Week 1 deliverable** (not vague "we help manage volunteers")
   - "Migrate your volunteer applications to digital intake + auto-sorting within 5 days"
   - "Automated shift scheduling for your 24/7 emergency operations"
   - "Volunteer engagement dashboard showing who's going quiet, auto-reminders for lapsed volunteers"

3. **Reference proof** (ClinicalHours)
   - "I built ClinicalHours—a volunteer management platform for clinics. Our pilot went from spreadsheet chaos to 30-second digital check-ins."
   - Mention specific features used by BCS FHC: applicant tracking, hour logging, interview coordination
   - Founder credibility: "I understand the clinic side; I've built this for exactly your use case"

4. **Position as operator, not vendor** (you understand their world, not selling at them)
   - Close: "Can we schedule 15 minutes to see if this fits your workflow?" (low-pressure, collaborative tone)
   - No sales pitch language; pure operational discussion

## Target Customer Profile (From Internship Success Factors)

**Internship success:** Non-technical founders with clear operational problems + Series A+ funding

**Clinic equivalent:**
- Clinic director/founder or volunteer coordinator (direct decision-maker)
- 50+ active volunteers (clear pain signal at scale)
- Free clinic or nonprofit primary care (mission-driven, not VC-backed = different budget dynamics)
- Manual volunteer management (using Forms, spreadsheets, email, phone)
- Established enough to have budget ($1,200+/year) but small enough for direct founder contact

**Why this profile:**
- Founders/directors hire on capability, not credentials (ClinicalHours is your proof)
- They have immediate, measurable problems (volunteer chaos)
- Manual processes are entrenched (high switching cost once converted, but low technical switching cost to get there)
- They're willing to pay for solutions that save time (volunteer coordinator salary >> ClinicalHours cost)

## Personalization Deep Dive

### Research Phase (Pre-Email)

For each clinic, answer these before writing the email:

1. **Operational Pain Signal** (Why this clinic, now)
   - How many volunteers? (Indicator: >50 = chaos, high pain)
   - What's their mission? (Emergency = 24/7 pain; pregnancy center = retention pain; homeless = screening pain)
   - What hours do they operate? (24/7 = scheduling nightmare; fixed hours = less pain)
   - Any visible "we're hiring volunteers" signals? (Recent website updates, social media posts = active recruitment = turnover)

2. **Likely Current Process** (What they're dealing with)
   - Visit their website > volunteer page > what forms do they use? (PDF, paper, Google Form?)
   - Any visible process documentation? (Email about interview scheduling, volunteer coordinator job postings?)
   - Check LinkedIn: Do they have a "volunteer coordinator" role? (If yes = pain is large enough to hire for)

3. **Concrete Week 1 Offer** (Make it specific to their model)
   - **Emergency clinic:** "Automated shift scheduling for 24/7 coverage (volunteer availability matching)"
   - **Pregnancy center:** "Volunteer engagement dashboard + auto-reminders (keep peer counselors active)"
   - **Large clinic (140+ volunteers):** "Migrate current intake to digital + set up attendance tracking"
   - **Multi-location clinic:** "Clinic-specific volunteer scheduling (different forms, rules, roles per location)"
   - **New clinic:** "Build volunteer operations from scratch the right way (intake, scheduling, hour tracking)"

4. **Why Now** (Create urgency/relevance)
   - Volunteer season: Spring/summer volunteer surge (recruitment burden)
   - Budget cycle: Q4 (nonprofits plan year budgets; mention casually: "timing is good for year 1 pricing")
   - Competitor signal: If they just hired volunteer coordinator, operations pain is acute NOW

## Email Template (Problem-First)

**Subject Line:** `[Founder Name] — volunteer coordination solution built for clinics like [Clinic Name]`

**Body (Under 120 words):**

Paragraph 1 — **Problem Recognition** (show you understand their specific pain)
```
Hi [Name],

I looked at your volunteer program (140+ active volunteers is impressive). 
Managing that at scale with email + spreadsheets must be consuming 20+ 
hours/month from someone.
```

Paragraph 2 — **Specific Offer + Proof** (concrete deliverable + your credibility)
```
I built ClinicalHours—volunteer management platform for clinics. Our pilot 
went from manual scheduling chaos to digital intake in 5 days.

In that time, I can migrate your current volunteer process to a digital form 
that auto-sorts by clinic role. No switching costs—just better data.
```

Paragraph 3 — **The Ask** (low-friction, collaborative)
```
Can we schedule 15 minutes this week to see if this fits your workflow?
```

**Sign-off:** `Shivam Kanodia / clinicalhours.org | 214-470-0598`

## Sales Process (Post-Email Response)

**If clinic responds positively:**
1. **Qualification call** (15 minutes): "What's your current volunteer process? What breaks most often?"
2. **Demo** (20 minutes): Show ClinicalHours with their use case (volunteer intake, scheduling, hour logging)
3. **Pilot proposal** (written): "30-day free trial + implementation support. Goal: You see tangible time savings by end of month."
4. **Conversion:** Post-pilot, offer Standard tier at $1,200/year, month-to-month initially

**If clinic doesn't respond:**
- Follow up at Day 3 (short: "Just circling back in case my email got buried")
- Follow up at Day 7 (try alternate contact: "Wanted to reach volunteer coordinator if possible")
- Mark for re-contact in 2 months (maybe new fiscal year, new budget cycle)

## Why This Works vs. Generic Outreach

1. **Founder-to-founder communication** (you've built what they need; you understand their pain)
2. **Specificity signals respect** (you researched them; not a mass email)
3. **Concrete deliverable removes risk** (Week 1 outcome is tangible, not vague)
4. **Low switching cost** (easy to say yes; low commitment to try)
5. **High lock-in post-implementation** (once integrated into operations, clinic doesn't want to revert)
