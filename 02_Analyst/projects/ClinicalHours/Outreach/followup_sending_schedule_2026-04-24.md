## Response Classification Guide

### Positive Responses (Schedule demo/call)
- "I'm interested in learning more..."
- "Can you send me more information..."
- "When are you available to talk?"
- "This looks relevant to our situation..."
- Direct positive interest signals

**Action:** Reply within 24 hours with specific meeting options (Calendly link or 3 time slots)

### Neutral Responses (Nurture sequence)
- "Interesting, I'll take a look..."
- "Maybe something for the future..."
- "We're exploring options..."
- Generic acknowledgment without commitment

**Action:** Add to nurture sequence, follow up in 2 weeks with new angle

### Negative Responses (Log and archive)
- "We're not interested..."
- "Not a fit for us right now..."
- "We use [competitor] already..."
- Explicit "no thanks"

**Action:** Log as "explicit decline," mark for 6-month re-engagement attempt

### Non-Response (Follow-up v2)
- No response after 10 days
- Email likely read but not replied

**Action:** Send third-touch email (Day 14) with different angle

## Demo Scheduling Process

**For positive responders:**

1. **Same-day reply** (within 24 hours of response)
2. **Subject:** "Re: [Original subject] — Let's find a time"
3. **Content:**
   - Thank them for interest
   - Offer 3 specific time slots (15-minute meetings)
   - Calendly link as backup option
   - Brief agenda ("I'll show you how ClinicalHours works for clinics your size")

4. **Scheduling preference:** Calendar invite + backup email
5. **Meeting setup:** 
   - Zoom link in calendar invite
   - Slide deck ready (quick 10-min overview + 5-min Q&A)
   - Sample clinic data loaded (anonymized)
   - BCS case study metrics pulled up

## Second Follow-Up (v2) Strategy

**Trigger:** No response 10 days after follow-up send  
**Timing:** Send Day 14 (2026-04-28 for April batch)

**Approach:** Different angle + urgency

**Sample v2 Email:**

---

Hi [Clinic],

Quick note: I'm checking in one more time on ClinicalHours because I think there's a real opportunity here for your clinic.

[New angle: Different value prop OR introduce new urgency OR add social proof]

I'm usually easy to reach at [phone]. If phone is easier, happy to chat that way.

Thanks,
Shivam

---

**V2 Angles (rotate by clinic type):**
- Case study with specific metrics
- Social proof ("3 other [clinic type] clinics in your region...")
- Lower-friction ask ("Just a 10-min call")
- Phone number offer (direct outreach signal)

---

## Escalation to Phone (Day 21+)

**Trigger:** No response to email v1 + v2  
**Decision:** Phone outreach or archive

**Phone approach:**
1. Call clinic main number
2. Ask for volunteer coordinator or clinic director by name
3. Keep pitch to 60 seconds (not a full demo)
4. Goal: Schedule 15-min video call
5. If no answer: Voicemail with callback number
