# DFW Clinic Outreach Strategy

**Goal:** Win 2-3 of 4 tier 1 DFW clinics by end of Q2 2026. Each clinic represents $1,200-1,500 ARR + reference customer for national expansion.

**Tier 1 Targets (DFW):**
1. **Mercy Clinic Friends** — 140+ volunteers
2. **Julia's Center** — 60+ volunteers
3. **Community Health** — 60+ volunteers
4. **EPIC Medical** — 62+ volunteers

---

## Persona 1: Operations Director (Decision-Maker)

**Profile:**
- **Role:** Manages volunteer programs, recruitment, retention, compliance
- **Seniority:** Director / Program Manager level
- **Title variations:** Director of Volunteer Services, Operations Manager, Community Health Program Director
- **Budget authority:** Yes (controls or influences $1-5K annual software spend)
- **Influence:** High — their pain directly drives adoption decision

**Typical Background:**
- 10-20 years in nonprofit/healthcare operations
- Started as volunteer coordinator; promoted into ops role
- Often wears multiple hats (HR, volunteer management, compliance)
- Tech-savvy but not technical (uses Salesforce, Quickbooks, Google Workspace)

**Current Pain Points:**
1. **Recruitment friction** — Receives 30-50 volunteer applications/month but manual review takes 4-6 hours
2. **No visibility into volunteer pool** — Relies on emails + spreadsheets to track who's qualified, when they're available, compliance status
3. **Volunteer retention is bleeding** — Loses 20-30% annually; doesn't track why (missing follow-up, unclear scheduling, lack of engagement)
4. **Compliance risk** — No automated way to verify volunteer backgrounds, hours logged, or clinical requirements (where applicable)
5. **Time cost** — Spends 5-8 hours/week on manual volunteer admin; that's 20% of their time that could go to program growth

**Current Tools Stack:**
- Google Forms for applications (no scoring/ranking)
- Google Sheets for volunteer database (manual updates)
- Email for communication (no templates, no scheduling)
- Manual hours tracking (if at all)
- Possibly Salesforce but under-configured

**Success Metric They Care About:**
- **Recruit 20% more volunteers with same effort** (or less)
- **Reduce admin time by 50%**
- **Track volunteer hours accurately for grant reporting** (many free clinics need this for funders)

**Decision Process:**
- Researches solutions → asks 3-5 similar clinics for recommendations → requests demo/trial
- Cares about: cost, ease of setup, mobile-friendly (volunteer coordinators work on phone), integration with existing tools
- Price sensitivity: $1,200/year feels reasonable; $3K+ feels expensive
- Doesn't want another "tool to manage" — wants something that just works

**Objections They'll Raise:**
- "We've been using Forms and Sheets for 5 years, it works fine" (inertia)
- "What if you go out of business?" (startup risk)
- "Our volunteers don't use tech much" (UX concern)
- "We'd need buy-in from our volunteer coordinators" (change management)

**What They Want to Hear:**
- Specific time savings (not vague "streamline operations")
- How other clinics your size use it (social proof)
- Data security + compliance story (HIPAA-ready, background check integrations)
- Easy onboarding (not a 6-week implementation)

## Persona 2: Volunteer Coordinator (Power User)

**Profile:**
- **Role:** Day-to-day volunteer management, recruitment, scheduling, follow-up
- **Seniority:** Coordinator or Associate level
- **Title variations:** Volunteer Coordinator, Program Coordinator, Community Outreach Coordinator
- **Budget authority:** No (recommends to Operations Director)
- **Influence:** Very high — if they hate using it, adoption fails
- **Typical workload:** 20-30 hours/week on volunteer admin

**Typical Background:**
- 3-8 years in volunteer coordination (often promoted from volunteer role)
- Core skill: people management (recruiting, onboarding, retention, problem-solving)
- Moderate tech skills (spreadsheets OK, but prefers simple UI)
- Probably manages 50-150 active volunteers

**Current Pain Points:**
1. **Manual application screening** — Reads 100+ form submissions/month, copies data into spreadsheet manually
2. **Scheduling chaos** — No visibility into who's available when; sends many "is anyone free on Tuesday?" emails
3. **Communication overload** — Texts, emails, WhatsApp, calls all mixed together; hard to track who needs what
4. **Volunteer ghosting** — People disappear without explanation; no way to re-engage automatically
5. **Hours tracking is manual** — Volunteers text/email hours; coordinator logs them manually
6. **Onboarding takes forever** — Each new volunteer needs custom paperwork + orientation; no templates

**Current Tools Stack:**
- Google Forms (application collection)
- Google Sheets (database management, with lots of manual copying)
- Email + texting (communication)
- Phone calls (rescheduling, follow-ups)
- Possibly a shared Google Drive folder (chaos)

**Success Metric They Care About:**
- **Spend less time on data entry and manual follow-up**
- **See which volunteers are available before sending blast emails**
- **Never lose a volunteer application because it got buried**
- **Know how many hours each volunteer logged this month** (no manual math)

**Decision Process:**
- Doesn't decide, but their opinion heavily influences the Director
- Demo is critical: they need to see themselves using it immediately
- Cares about: speed, intuitive UI (doesn't want to watch training videos), mobile access
- Will push back if it requires lots of extra work from them ("one more thing I have to manage")

**Objections They'll Raise:**
- "This looks complicated, I don't have time to learn new tools" (friction)
- "Our volunteers won't fill out another form/app" (adoption burden)
- "I still have to do manual follow-up anyway?" (if automation is incomplete)

**What They Want to Hear:**
- "This will save you 5+ hours/week"
- "Your volunteers apply once and you never need to ask for info again"
- "Mobile app so you can recruit from anywhere"
- "One-click communication to available volunteers"

## Weakpoint Analysis: DFW Clinic Landscape

**Common Weaknesses (Across All 4 Targets):**

| Weakness | Impact | How ClinicalHours Solves It |
|----------|--------|-----|
| **Manual volunteer onboarding** | Takes 2-4 hours per new volunteer | Automated application → interview invitation → onboarding workflow |
| **No volunteer scheduling visibility** | Coordinators send "who's available?" emails; bad experience | Volunteers set availability; coordinators see calendar |
| **Lost applications** | Submissions in email/Forms get lost in inbox | Centralized application queue with status tracking |
| **Hours tracking is manual** | No audit trail; hard for grant reporting | Volunteers log hours in platform; reports auto-generated |
| **Retention bleeding at 20-30%/year** | Expensive to constantly recruit new volunteers | Engagement features (recognition, scheduling preferences, communication) reduce churn |
| **No compliance tracking** | Risky for HIPAA/screening; hard for audits | Background check integrations, compliance dashboards |
| **Coordinator burnout** | High turnover in coordinator role | Reduce admin burden from 20-30 hrs/week to 10-15 hrs/week |

## Competitive Landscape for DFW Clinics

**Current Volunteer Management Status Quo (Best Guess from Memory):**

- **Mercy Clinic Friends:** Likely Google Forms + Sheets (mature nonprofit, but volunteer ops is secondary)
- **Julia's Center:** Similar setup; possibly some inherited system from larger parent org
- **Community Health:** Likely completely manual (email + spreadsheet)
- **EPIC Medical:** Likely Google Forms or similar

**Known Competitors in Space:**
- **Volgistics** — $50-100/month, overkill for free clinic, enterprise-focused, no free clinic customers visible
- **VSys** — Mostly hospital/large nonprofit networks; not positioned for free clinics
- **Benevity** — Enterprise ESG tool, $5K+/year, totally wrong fit

**Why ClinicalHours Wins:** Pricing + niche focus + ease of setup (not complex systems designed for hospital networks).

## Outreach Sequence (Multi-Touch)

**Phase 1: Research & Prep (Before Outreach)**
- [ ] Verify target clinics' volunteer counts (via HRSA data or website)
- [ ] Identify Operations Director + Volunteer Coordinator names (LinkedIn, clinic website)
- [ ] Find their volunteer application process (Google Forms URL, phone number)
- [ ] Research their recent news/funding (website, news section, LinkedIn)

**Phase 2: Warm Touch (Cold Email)**
- Email subject: "[Clinic Name] volunteer coordinators — 5 hours/week back"
- Body: 3-sentence hook → 1-sentence product pitch → clinic-specific insight → 15-min call CTA
- Personalization: Mention their clinic name + volunteer count + specific pain point we observed
- Sign-off: Shivam / clinicalhours.org

**Phase 3: Follow-Up (Email #2, 5 days later)**
- If no response: "Quick thought on [specific problem they probably have]"
- Shorter than first email; different angle (maybe mention time savings or competitor setup)

**Phase 4: Warm Outreach (LinkedIn, if available)**
- Connect with Volunteer Coordinator
- Message: "Hey [Name], saw you're managing [X] volunteers at [clinic]. We help coordinators cut admin time in half. Free to chat?"

**Phase 5: Demo/Pilot**
- 15-min call (from cold email CTA)
- Screen share demo of ClinicalHours → show how their workflow improves
- Offer 30-day free trial
- Close conversation: "Would you be open to a trial with 2-3 volunteers?"

## Messaging Framework

**For Operations Director:**
- **Hook:** "We help clinic ops directors get 5-8 hours back every week"

- **Solution:** "ClinicalHours automates applications, scheduling, and hours tracking"
- **Proof:** "Our first clinic (BCS FHC) saved $X in admin time in first 30 days"
- **CTA:** "15-minute call to see how we'd work for your clinic?"

**For Volunteer Coordinator:**
- **Hook:** "Stop copying form data into spreadsheets"
- **Problem:** "You get 30-50 applications/month but spend 4-6 hours just screening them"
- **Solution:** "Automatic application intake → interview scheduling → hours tracking"
- **Proof:** "Coordinators at our current clinic freed up 5+ hours/week"
- **CTA:** "Want a quick walkthrough (15 min)?"
