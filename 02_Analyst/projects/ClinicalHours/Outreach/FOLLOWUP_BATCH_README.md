## Key Success Factors

### What makes a follow-up email work?

1. **Different angle from first email** — Not repetitive
2. **New value signal** — Case study, peer reference, new insight
3. **Softer tone** — "Just checking in" vs "final chance to respond"
4. **Specific CTA** — 3 concrete time options, not "let me know"
5. **Under 120 words** — Clinic directors are busy
6. **Personalization** — Clinic name + role-specific pain point

### What kills response rates?

1. **Generic templates** — "Hi [Clinic], here's our product..."
2. **Long emails** — More than 150 words
3. **Vague CTAs** — "Let me know if you're interested"
4. **No social proof** — "We're the best" with no evidence
5. **Weak subject lines** — "Follow-up" or generic greetings
6. **Late timing** — Following up 2+ weeks later (use 3-5 days)

## Common Questions

### Q: What if I get a response from someone other than the original contact?
**A:** Great! They're forwarding to the decision-maker. Treat as warm lead, schedule demo.

### Q: How long should I wait before sending follow-up v2 (third email)?
**A:** If no response after 10 days → Send second follow-up. If no response after 10 more days → Phone call or archive.

### Q: Should I personalize the subject line or keep it simple?
**A:** Personalize. "Re: ClinicalHours" → "Re: ClinicalHours—[Clinic name]" increases open rate by 15-20%.

### Q: What if a clinic asks pricing immediately?
**A:** Good sign! They're interested. Respond with pricing for their likely volunteer size, and emphasize 30-day trial option.

### Q: Can I send to multiple contacts at the same clinic?
**A:** Avoid if possible (looks spammy). Stick to volunteer coordinator / clinic director only.
