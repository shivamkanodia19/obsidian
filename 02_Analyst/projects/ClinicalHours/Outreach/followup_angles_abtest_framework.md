# Follow-Up Email A/B Testing Framework

**Objective:** Identify which follow-up angles generate highest response rates and positive conversion  
**Test window:** April 24 - May 1 (7 days post-send)  
**Success metric:** Response rate + Response quality (positive/neutral/negative breakdown)

---

## Follow-Up Angles (8 Total Tested)

### Angle 1: BCS Free Health Clinic Case Study
**Hypothesis:** Mentioning active customer success case increases credibility and perceived relevance  
**Subject Line Variants:**
- "One thing I should have mentioned about volunteer coordination..."
- "Following up—volunteer coordination solution for [Clinic]"

**Email Body Structure:**
- Reference previous email
- Introduce BCS case study: "We just helped BCS Free Health Clinic cut their volunteer coordination time significantly"
- Bridge to clinic relevance: "Since you're managing similar workflows..."
- CTA: 15-minute call to show what's working

**Clinics Testing (Target: 5-6 clinics):**
- Hope Clinic of McKinney
- Hope Clinic of Garland
- Christ's Family Clinic
- ICNA Relief Medical Clinic Dallas
- BCS Free Health Clinic (active conversation variant)
- Julia's Center

**Expected outcome:** Higher response rate (25%+) due to social proof signal

---

### Angle 2: Regional/Peer Reference
**Hypothesis:** Mentioning "other clinics in your region" creates peer pressure + relevance  
**Subject Line Variants:**
- "Quick follow-up: [Clinic] + volunteer hour tracking"
- "Re: ClinicalHours—volunteer platform"

**Email Body Structure:**
- Reference previous email
- Introduce peer signal: "I was talking with another [clinic type] in [region] yesterday..."
- Highlight specific pain point they mentioned
- Bridge to solution: "That's the problem ClinicalHours solves..."
- CTA: 15-minute call

**Clinics Testing (Target: 5-6 clinics):**
- GRACE Community Clinic (DFW region)
- Woven Health Clinic (DFW region)
- Mission Waco Health Clinic (Central Texas)
- Lubbock Impact (West Texas)
- Bethesda Health Clinic (North Texas)
- San José Clinic (South Texas)

**Expected outcome:** Moderate-high response rate (22-25%) via peer credibility

**Clinics Testing (Target: 2 clinics):**
- BCS Free Health Clinic (pilot active)
- Agape Clinic (active conversation)

**Expected outcome:** 100% response expected (warm lead); focus on conversion, not outreach

## Response Quality Rubric

### Score System (For Tracking Response Quality)

**Positive (Score: +1)**
- Explicit interest: "I'm interested in learning more"
- Meeting request: "When are you available?"
- Engagement signal: "Can you send me a demo link?"
- Domain expert question: "Does ClinicalHours handle [specific feature]?"

**Neutral (Score: 0)**
- Generic acknowledgment: "Thanks, I'll take a look"
- Vague interest: "Maybe something for the future"
- Information request: "Send me more details"
- Passing to colleague: "I'll forward this to [person]"

**Negative (Score: -1)**
- Explicit decline: "Not interested"
- Competitor mention: "We already use [Volgistics/VSys]"
- Wrong fit: "Doesn't match our current needs"
- Unsubscribe: "Remove me from your list"

**Quality metric:** Weighted average score per angle

## Hypothesis Validation Framework

**For each angle, determine:**

1. **Response Rate** = (Responses / Sent) × 100
2. **Positive Rate** = (Positive Responses / Total Responses) × 100
3. **Quality Score** = (Sum of response scores / # responses)
4. **Recommendation** = Use for next batch? Refine? Archive?

**Threshold logic:**

| Response Rate | Quality Score | Recommendation |
|---------------|---------------|-----------------|
| 25%+ | 0.5+ | **WINNER** — Use heavily in next batch |
| 20-24% | 0.3-0.5 | **STRONG** — Use in next batch with minor refinements |
| 15-19% | 0.0-0.3 | **MODERATE** — Test further or refine messaging |
| <15% | <0.0 | **WEAK** — Archive or redesign completely |

## Next-Batch Optimization

**Based on Week 1 results, for Week 2 outreach:**

1. **Double down on winning angles** (25%+ response rate)
2. **Refine moderate performers** (15-24% response rate)
3. **Archive/redesign weak angles** (<15% response rate)
4. **Test new subject line variants** for top-performing angles
5. **Increase volume** on highest-converting clinic types

**Example optimization logic:**
- If "Retention Focus" angle gets 28% response rate → Use for next 20 clinics
- If "Feature-Specific" gets 18% response rate → Refine messaging, test on 5 new clinics
- If "Multilingual" angle gets 12% response rate → Archive for this campaign, revisit in Q2

## Statistical Significance (Optional)

If testing with statistically valid sample sizes:

- **Minimum sample per angle:** 5-6 clinics (small-sample testing)
- **Confidence level:** 80%+ (acceptable for startup iteration)
- **Significance threshold:** 5%+ difference between angles

**Example:** If Angle 1 gets 28% response and Angle 2 gets 18%, that's a 10% absolute difference (likely meaningful for optimization decisions).
