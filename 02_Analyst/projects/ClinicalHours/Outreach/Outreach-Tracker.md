## Contact Research Checklist

For each clinic, before email goes out:

- [ ] Founder/director name verified
- [ ] Volunteer coordinator name + email found (or director email if coordinator unknown)
- [ ] Website researched for current volunteer process signals
- [ ] LinkedIn check: Recent volunteer coordinator hires? (indicates pain level)
- [ ] Email tone: Personalized to their mission + pain signal
- [ ] Subject line: Customized, not generic
- [ ] Proof point: Which ClinicalHours feature most relevant to them?
- [ ] Week 1 deliverable: Specific + concrete to their use case

## Notes & Learnings

### From Internship Email Strategy
- **Specificity wins:** Mentioning specific problems (140+ volunteers, 24/7 operations) gets attention
- **Proof over credentials:** "I built this for you" > "I want to work here"
- **Concrete deliverable:** "Week 1 outcome" > "I want to learn"
- **Founder emails work:** Direct reach to decision-maker = higher response

### To Apply to Clinics
- Research before sending (show you did homework)
- Lead with problem recognition (show you understand their pain)
- Mention ClinicalHours proof (BCS FHC is live proof)
- Position as operator, not vendor
- Keep emails under 120 words (clinic directors are busy)
