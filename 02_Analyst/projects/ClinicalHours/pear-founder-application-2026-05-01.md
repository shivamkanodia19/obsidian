# Pear Founder Application Notes - 2026-05-01

Status: backfilled memo from the Pear VC application-writing session.

## Core Decision

The strongest founder answer was not the generic "novel person" angle. It was the ClinicalHours story.

The useful framing from the session:

- Start with the original marketplace thesis.
- Show where reality contradicted the clean story.
- Explain the pivots as evidence of pattern recognition, not randomness.
- End on the founder behavior: update fast when the system breaks.

## Best Positioning

The cleanest line of attack was:

"In a three-founder team, I am usually the person who notices when the company we are describing is not the company customers actually want."

From there, the best supporting story was:

- ClinicalHours began as a marketplace for pre-med students seeking clinical hours.
- The real bottleneck turned out to be inside clinics, where volunteer coordination lived in spreadsheets, paper forms, and phone calls.
- The product shifted toward workflow infrastructure for clinics.
- Even that was not enough, so the product moved further up the stack toward automation that removed repetitive admin work.
- Distribution then became the next bottleneck, which led to mapping `50+` clinics across BCS and DFW and building a repeatable outreach engine.

## What To Avoid

- generic phrases like "next-generation tools" or "save time and money"
- sounding like a pitch deck instead of an earned founder story
- overusing the word "outlier" instead of demonstrating the behavior

## Backup Angle

The bench-press story still worked as a secondary answer because it showed diagnosis over brute force:

- plateau
- identify the real bottleneck
- track the system closely
- change the method instead of just adding effort

But the Pear session conclusion was that ClinicalHours is the stronger lead answer because it is more obviously founder-specific and better tied to real customer feedback.
