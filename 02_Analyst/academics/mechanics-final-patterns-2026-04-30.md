# TAMU Mechanics Final Patterns - 2026-04-30

Source artifact: [[01_Source/academics/tamu_mech_finals/analysis]]

## Summary

The mechanics finals from `2007-2019` were scraped and reviewed to find recurring structure and topic patterns. The clean takeaway is that the exam stories change, but the underlying physics toolkit repeats heavily.

## Durable Patterns

- Every final is free-response and emphasizes showing work for partial credit.
- Momentum shows up constantly through collisions, explosions, sticking problems, and moving-platform setups.
- Rotational dynamics is nearly as common as linear momentum.
- Springs, SHM, and pendulum-style small-angle reasoning appear over and over.
- Friction is often the trickiest part of the problem, especially when it is variable or tied to slipping thresholds.
- Multi-topic blends are common, especially collision plus oscillator, rotation plus friction, or energy plus non-constant force.
- Setup quality matters more than arithmetic speed. Several problems reward equation-building and free-body diagrams more than finishing every computation.

## Best Study Targets

- 1D and 2D momentum conservation
- rotational dynamics with pulleys and torques
- spring motion and SHM setup
- friction-heavy Newton's law problems on inclines or stacked blocks
- work-energy with non-constant forces
- central-force and gravitational potential problems

## Practical Use

If studying under time pressure, the highest-return move is to drill the repeated mechanics templates rather than memorize the surface stories. The best preparation is being able to recognize the hidden category quickly and set up the governing equations cleanly.
