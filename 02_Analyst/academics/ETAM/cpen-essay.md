# Computer Engineering Essay

## Source Version (from /01_Source/)

I want to do Computer Engineering because the world is becoming more and less technical at the same time. The barrier of entry is basically at the floor, but to win in your field, it's never been higher.

I built ClinicalHours as a two-sided marketplace that has since evolved into an agency providing volunteer coordination infrastructure to smaller clinics. I had to ask myself questions beyond a basic, technical engineering perspective: How do you design a system in which data flows from clinics to volunteers and back? How can our system truly benefit clinics while saving them time? How do we implement the key features that they need? These are design questions, not code questions, and I strongly believe that answering them well is what sets good engineers apart from bad ones. 

My economics research hits the same architectural constraints. I work on a futures forecasting dashboard for cattle operators. It's a data pipeline that handles real-time market streams, a frontend that clearly surfaces uncertainty, and backend systems that manage the computational load. Every piece depends on how hardware and software communicate.

Computer Engineering is the bridge between these layers. Understanding how hardware and software interact is the key to making a successful system. 

That's what I need to do for this work to go well. ClinicalHours showed me what operational rigor requires. CPEN teaches me the technical foundation to build systems that scale.

## Strategic Recommendations (CPEN Admissions Lens)

**What CPEN committee wants:** Engineers who understand that systems work because hardware and software are designed together, not separately. Architectural thinking grounded in both domains.

### Strengths in this essay
✅ Opening hook about rising technical barriers is memorable  
✅ "Design questions, not code questions" — exactly what CPEN values  
✅ "Sets good engineers apart from bad ones" — shows you understand quality  
✅ Concrete design questions (data flow, computational load, feature implementation)  
✅ Two examples (ClinicalHours + forecasting) show pattern across domains

### Strategic edits to consider

1. **Open stronger on the technical angle** — Your current opening is philosophical but CPEN committee wants to see technical depth:
   - Current: "The barrier of entry is basically at the floor, but to win in your field, it's never been higher"
   - This is true but abstract. Consider grounding it:
   - Stronger: "Computer Engineering appeals because the problems that matter aren't about coding speed or syntax. They're about understanding how hardware constraints shape software design, and vice versa."

2. **Add specificity about CPEN curriculum** — Your essay mentions "hardware and software communicate" but could name specific CPEN domains:
   - Embedded systems (ClinicalHours could involve mobile/embedded considerations)
   - Real-time systems (cattle operator dashboard has time-sensitive constraints)
   - Digital design or circuit-level thinking (how electrical design shapes system limits)
   - This shows you've researched the program

3. **Reframe "architectural constraints"** — You use this phrase twice, which works, but CPEN might interpret "architecture" as software architecture. Consider being more specific:
   - "These hit the same constraints: hardware limitations, real-time processing, interfacing between systems"
   - Shows you think about the full stack

4. **Strengthen the closing** — Current closing is solid but could emphasize CPEN-specific skills:
   - Current: "CPEN teaches me the technical foundation to build systems that scale"
   - Stronger: "CPEN teaches me to design systems where hardware and software constraints become opportunities, not limitations"

5. **Word count issue** — Your source is ~250 words, essay limit is 250. You're at the edge. Could trim slightly if revising.

### Optional: Add a specific technical question

- "How do you design a real-time pipeline where latency matters (cattle operators need forecasts fast)?"
- "How does the choice between embedded systems vs. cloud computing affect our ability to reach rural operators?"
