## 2007 EXAM ✅ FULLY ANALYZED

**Date:** November 20, 2007  
**Extraction Status:** ✅ Complete text extraction

### Problem 1: Polar Coordinates (25 pts)

**1a. Derivation (20 pts)** - Easy-Medium
- **Topics:** Kinematics, polar coordinates
- **Task:** Derive $v_r$, $v_\theta$, $a_r$, $a_\theta$
- **Key Formulas:**
  - $v_r = \dot{r}$, $v_\theta = r\dot{\theta}$
  - $a_r = \ddot{r} - r\dot{\theta}^2$, $a_\theta = r\ddot{\theta} + 2\dot{r}\dot{\theta}$

**1b. Force Calculation (5 pts)** - Medium
- **Given:** $r(t) = kt^2$, $\theta(t) = bt^2$, mass m
- **Find:** Force F(t)
- **Method:** Calculate velocity → acceleration → apply F=ma

### Problem 2: Collisions (25 pts)

**2a. Inelastic Collision (~12 pts)** - Medium
- **Setup:** Ball swings, hits object, sticks
- **Find:** Height reached after collision
- **Topics:** Energy conservation (swing), momentum conservation (collision)

**2b. Elastic Collision (~13 pts)** - Easy-Medium
- **Setup:** Ball bounces off object
- **Find:** Write equations (don't solve)
- **Topics:** Elastic collision, momentum + energy conservation

### Problem 3: Angular Momentum (25 pts)

**3a. Torque (~8 pts)** - Medium
- **Setup:** Man on platform with weights
- **Find:** Force to spin platform
- **Topics:** Torque, angular acceleration

**3b. Arms Pull In (~8 pts)** - Easy-Medium
- **Find:** Angular velocity when arms pull from S to S/4
- **Topics:** Angular momentum conservation
- **Key:** $L = I\omega = constant$, moment of inertia decreases

**3c. With Man's Mass (~8 pts)** - Medium
- **Change:** Add man's moment of inertia
- **Find:** Final angular velocity
- **Topics:** Composite moment of inertia

### Problem 4: Rotating Rod (25 pts)

**4a. Horizontal Torque (~6 pts)** - Easy
- **Find:** Gravity's torque when rod is horizontal
- **Answer:** $\tau = MgS/2$

**4b. Horizontal Acceleration (~6 pts)** - Easy-Medium
- **Find:** Acceleration of right end
- **Topics:** Torque, angular acceleration, moment of inertia

**4c. Vertical Torque (~6 pts)** - Easy
- **Find:** Gravity's torque when rod is vertical
- **Answer:** $\tau = 0$ (force acts through pivot)

**4d. Vertical Acceleration (~7 pts)** - Hard
- **Find:** Acceleration when rod is vertical
- **Topics:** Centripetal vs. tangential acceleration
- **Key Insight:** Changes from 4b due to geometry

### 2007 Summary

- **Topics:** Polar kinematics (25), collisions (25), angular momentum (25), rotational dynamics (25)
- **Difficulty:** Easy 24 pts, Easy-Med 56 pts, Medium 40 pts
- **Total:** 100 pts, 50 minutes

## 2008 EXAM ✅ FULLY ANALYZED

**Extraction Status:** ✅ Complete text extraction

### Problem 1: Polar Coordinates (25 pts) - Easy-Medium

Same format as 2007 Problem 1a (full derivation).

### Problem 2: Sled & Collision (25 pts)

**2a. Energy Conserv (~12 pts)** - Easy
- **Setup:** Man + sled on frictionless hill
- **Find:** Velocity at bottom
- **Topics:** Gravitational potential energy

**2b. Post-Collision Equations (~13 pts)** - Medium-Hard
- **Setup:** Man jumps between sleds after reaching bottom
- **Find:** Write position equations (don't solve)
- **Topics:** Momentum conservation, 2D kinematics

### Problem 3: Angular Momentum with Variable I (25 pts)

**3a. $\omega(t)$ (~8 pts)** - Hard
- **Setup:** Mass moves toward center, I changes with time
- **Find:** Angular velocity as function of time
- **Topics:** Angular momentum conservation with changing I

**3b. Force (~8 pts)** - Medium-Hard
- **Find:** Force exerted by rod on moving mass
- **Topics:** Centripetal force, kinematics

**3c. With Massive Rod (~8 pts)** - Medium-Hard
- **Find:** $\omega(t)$ with rod having moment of inertia
- **Topics:** Composite moment of inertia

### Problem 4: Electron-Proton System (25 pts)

**4a. Angular Momentum (~6 pts)** - Easy-Medium
- **Setup:** Electron orbits proton at radius R
- **Find:** Angular momentum L
- **Topics:** Circular motion, central force

**4b. Kinetic Energy (~7 pts)** - Easy-Medium
- **Given:** $r(t) = r(0) + c_1t$, $\theta(t) = \theta(0) + c_2t$
- **Find:** Kinetic energy
- **Topics:** Polar coordinate velocity

**4c. Work by Force (~7 pts)** - Easy
- **Find:** Work by Coulomb force
- **Topics:** Conservative forces, potential energy

### 2008 Summary

- **Topics:** Polar kinematics (25), sled (25), angular momentum (25), central forces (25)
- **Notable:** Introduces 2D collision, variable moment of inertia, central forces
- **Total:** 100 pts, 50 minutes

## 2009 EXAM ✅ FULLY ANALYZED

**Extraction Status:** ✅ Complete text extraction

### Problem 1: Polar Coordinates (25 pts) - Easy-Medium

Derive r and θ components of velocity and acceleration.

### Problem 2: Block & Spring Collision (25 pts)

**2a. Elastic (~12 pts)** - Medium
- **Setup:** Block on frictionless table, spring attached, object hits it
- **Find:** Maximum spring compression after elastic collision
- **Topics:** Elastic collision, spring PE

**2b. Inelastic (~13 pts)** - Medium
- **Setup:** Object sticks to block
- **Find:** Maximum spring compression after inelastic collision
- **Topics:** Inelastic collision, spring PE

### Problem 3: Conveyor Belt (25 pts)

**3a. Velocity (~8 pts)** - Easy-Medium
- **Setup:** Box on circular conveyor, belt has angular acceleration $\alpha = ct$
- **Find:** Velocity when reaching quarter-circle
- **Topics:** Rotational kinematics with varying acceleration

**3b. Force vs. Time (~8 pts)** - Medium
- **Find:** Force from belt on box as function of time
- **Topics:** Tangential + centripetal forces, both changing

**3c. Maximum Acceleration (~9 pts)** - Medium-Hard
- **Given:** Coefficient of friction μ
- **Find:** Maximum c for no slipping
- **Topics:** Friction limit, circular motion constraint

### Problem 4: Angular Momentum & Torque (25 pts)

**Part 1: Flying Plane (10 pts)**

**4a. Angular Momentum (~5 pts)** - Easy
- **Find:** Plane's L about origin at position (x,y)
- **Formula:** $L = \vec{r} \times m\vec{v}$

**4b. Torque (~5 pts)** - Easy
- **Find:** Torque by gravity about origin
- **Formula:** $\tau = \vec{r} \times m\vec{g}$

**Part 2: Ejected Mass (15 pts)**

**4a. Angular Velocity (~8 pts)** - Medium
- **Setup:** Mass ejected perpendicular from rotating rod
- **Find:** Final angular velocity of rod
- **Topics:** Angular momentum conservation

**4b. With Massive Rod (~7 pts)** - Medium
- **Find:** Angular momentum with rod's I included
- **Topics:** Composite system

### 2009 Summary

- **Topics:** Polar kinematics (25), springs (25), conveyor belt (25), angular momentum (25)
- **Notable:** First exam with major spring focus
- **Total:** 100 pts, 50 minutes
