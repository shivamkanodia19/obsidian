## 2005 EXAM - Complete Analysis

**Format:** 4 problems, 100 pts, standard Physics 206 Exam 3 format

### Problem 1: Polar Kinematics (25 pts)

**Exact Wording:**
"Derive the expressions for the $\hat{r}$ and $\hat{\theta}$ components of the velocity and acceleration."

**Type:** Derivation (standard format - identical across all 15 years)
**Points:** 25
**Difficulty:** Easy-Medium
**Topics:** Polar coordinates, kinematics, vector calculus
**Key Formulas to Derive:**
- $v_r = \dot{r}$
- $v_\theta = r\dot{\theta}$
- $a_r = \ddot{r} - r\dot{\theta}^2$
- $a_\theta = r\ddot{\theta} + 2\dot{r}\dot{\theta}$

---

### Problem 2: Two Disks / Angular Momentum (25 pts)

**Setup (from page 2):**
Two disks on a frictionless axis. Disk 1 has moment of inertia $I_1$ and disk 2 has moment of inertia $I_2$. Both initially at rest on the same horizontal axis.

**Sub-parts identified:**
- Part a: Involves collision/impact between disks
- Part b: Angular momentum conservation
- Topics: Moment of inertia, angular momentum, collision

**Points:** Distributed across 2-3 sub-parts
**Difficulty:** Medium
**Key Concepts:**
- Angular momentum conservation: $L = I\omega = constant$
- Moment of inertia
- Rotational collisions
