# Expansion Playbook: Wave 4+ (50–70 Companies)

**Goal:** Scale from 20 verified Flower Mound companies to 50–70 companies across DFW metro (Dallas, Fort Worth, Arlington, Plano, Irving, Lewisville, Frisco, etc.) without losing email quality.

---

## Phase 1: Company Discovery (Rapid Filtering)

### Step 1: Define Target List by Industry

**Use industry frameworks from [[industry-frameworks]] to focus on:**
1. Healthcare facilities (nursing homes, clinics, hospitals)
2. Manufacturing (discrete, process, composites)
3. Logistics & warehousing (3PL, distribution centers)
4. Education (school districts, universities)
5. Government (cities, counties, utilities)
6. Business services (credentialing, staffing, HR)

**Geographic scope:** DFW metro (Dallas, Fort Worth, Arlington, Plano, Irving, Lewisville, Frisco, Carrollton, etc.)

---

### Step 2: Rapid Company Identification

**Use these free/low-cost sources to build target list:**

#### Healthcare Facilities
- **Source:** [CMS Medicare.gov Nursing Home Compare](https://www.medicare.gov/care-compare/) — Filter by Texas, DFW, then sort by deficiency count
- **Quick filter:** >20 health deficiencies OR staff turnover issues = qualifies
- **Contact:** Facility admin contact usually on CMS database

#### Manufacturing (Discrete + Process)
- **Source:** [Texas Manufacturers Association directory](https://www.txmfg.org/) OR [Chamber of Commerce directories](https://www.dallaschamber.org/)
- **Quick filter:** 50–500 employees (big enough to have ops problems, small enough to move fast)
- **Contact:** LinkedIn search for "VP Operations" + company name

#### Logistics & Warehousing
- **Source:** [CBRE Logistics Market Report](https://www.cbre.com/) OR Google Maps search "distribution center DFW"
- **Quick filter:** 3PL, distribution centers, last-mile facilities
- **Contact:** LinkedIn search for "Operations Manager" or "VP Logistics"

#### Education (School Districts)
- **Source:** [Texas Education Agency directory](https://tea.texas.gov/texas-schools/general-information/public-education-information-management-system) OR [TEA roster](https://tea.texas.gov/)
- **Quick filter:** Districts with declining enrollment (search local news + TEA reports)
- **Contact:** Superintendent email usually public via district website

#### Government & Municipal
- **Source:** City/county websites, [Government Jobs](https://www.governmentjobs.com/) OR Google "City of [X] government"
- **Quick filter:** Cities/counties 50K–500K population
- **Contact:** Town manager/city manager email usually public

#### Business Services
- **Source:** LinkedIn search "credentialing service" OR "staffing company" + DFW, or [Texas Business Network](https://www.dfw.org/)
- **Quick filter:** HR/staffing/credentialing firms with 20–200 employees
- **Contact:** CEO/founder LinkedIn or company website

---

### Step 3: Pre-Research (Rapid Qualification Filter)

**For each company, spend a few minutes answering:**

1. **Is this company real?** (Website up, recent news, hiring?)
2. **Do they likely have the pain point?** (Industry-specific signals)
3. **Is there a clear decision-maker?** (CEO, VP Ops, Superintendent, etc.)
4. **Can I find contact info?** (LinkedIn, website, business directory)

**If YES to all 4 → add to email list**  
**If NO → skip**

## Phase 2: Industry-Specific Research (Targeted Deep Dives)

**Goal:** Find 1–2 specific, verifiable operational issues per company (same methodology as Flower Mound research)

**Use this workflow for each company:**

### For Healthcare Facilities
1. Check [Medicare.gov Nursing Home Compare](https://www.medicare.gov/care-compare/) for deficiencies
2. Search [ProPublica Nursing Homes](https://projects.propublica.org/nursing-homes/) for specific issues
3. Check [Glassdoor reviews](https://www.glassdoor.com/) for "staff turnover" + facility name
4. Note: 1–2 specific issues from these sources
5. **Output:** "26 health deficiencies (CMS) + 80% turnover (Glassdoor)" → Email frame: compliance + retention

### For Manufacturing
1. Search Google + local news for "facility downtime" OR "production delays" + company name
2. Check [Glassdoor reviews](https://www.glassdoor.com/) for "downtime" OR "equipment issues" OR "production"
3. Check [Indeed company reviews](https://www.indeed.com/cmp/) for operational complaints
4. Note: 1–2 specific issues
5. **Output:** "20% downtime reported (Indeed reviews)" → Email frame: predictive maintenance

### For Logistics
1. Check [Glassdoor reviews](https://www.glassdoor.com/) for "staffing" OR "scheduling" OR "overtime"
2. Search local news for "hiring struggles" + logistics company
3. Check [LinkedIn posts/articles](https://www.linkedin.com/) from company for operational updates
4. Note: 1–2 specific issues
5. **Output:** "High staff turnover + overtime (Glassdoor)" → Email frame: demand forecasting

### For Education
1. Check [Texas Education Agency](https://tea.texas.gov/) for enrollment trends
2. Search local news for "school closure" OR "declining enrollment" + district name
3. Check district website for published enrollment forecasts or hiring freezes
4. Note: 1–2 specific issues
5. **Output:** "Declining enrollment + mid-year closure (local news)" → Email frame: enrollment forecasting

### For Government
1. Search city/county website for capital projects + budget documents
2. Google News for "project delays" OR "bond projects" + city name
3. Check municipal meeting notes (usually public) for project timeline issues
4. Note: 1–2 specific issues
5. **Output:** "$X bond projects + timeline delays (municipal documents)" → Email frame: project visibility

### For Business Services
1. Check [Glassdoor reviews](https://www.glassdoor.com/) for "cycle time" OR "efficiency" OR "process"
2. Search company website for case studies or client testimonials (often reveal pain points)
3. Check LinkedIn for founder/CEO updates on scaling challenges
4. Note: 1–2 specific issues
5. **Output:** "Long credentialing cycles (case studies)" → Email frame: cycle time compression

## Phase 3: Email Generation (Templates → Personalization)

**Goal:** Generate personalized emails using industry templates + company-specific data

### Step 1: Map Company to Industry + Template

Company: "Dallas Metro Distribution Center" (Logistics)  
Industry: Logistics & Warehousing  
Template: [[industry-frameworks#INDUSTRY 3: LOGISTICS & WAREHOUSING]]  
Decision-maker: Operations Manager / VP Logistics

### Step 2: Extract Company-Specific Data

Research findings:
- "High overtime costs reported in Glassdoor reviews"
- "Staffing shortages during peak seasons"
- "Scheduling based on last year's patterns"

## Phase 4: Contact Verification

**Before sending, verify email addresses:**

### For CEO/Executive Personal Emails
- Check [Hunter.io](https://hunter.io/) (10 credits free = 10 verifications)
- Check [RocketReach](https://www.rocketreach.co/) (free preview often shows email)
- Check LinkedIn (if no direct email, use LinkedIn message option)

### For Generic Company Emails (if no personal email found)
- Use company "Contact Us" form + note in email "Reaching out to operations leadership"
- Example: "info@company.com" for first outreach, then follow-up with LinkedIn DM to specific person

### For Public Sector (Government, Education)
- Usually have public emails on official websites
- Verify by calling main line + asking for correct email

---

## Phase 5: Sending & Tracking

### Sending Best Practices
- **Stagger sends:** Space out emails to avoid looking like spam
- **Track opens:** Use [Boomerang for Gmail](https://www.boomeranggmail.com/) (free tier) OR manual spreadsheet

### Follow-up Cadence
- **No open:** Resend with subject line variation
- **No reply after some time:** LinkedIn message (different angle)
- **Follow-up:** Phone call (if number available)

---

## Phase 6: Feedback Loop & Iteration

### Track Sending Results

**Metrics to monitor:**
1. **Open rate** (subject line effectiveness)
2. **Reply rate** (email framing effectiveness)
3. **Call booking rate** (CTA effectiveness)
4. **Industry response patterns** (which industries respond best?)

### Example Tracking Spreadsheet

| Company | Industry | Email Sent | Opened | Replied | Called | Notes |
|---------|----------|-----------|--------|---------|--------|-------|
| Cross Timbers | Healthcare | Yes | Yes | Yes | Pending | Strong response |
| IntelliCentrics | Healthcare | Yes | Yes | No | TBD | Opened, no reply yet |
| MI Windows | Manufacturing | Yes | No | No | TBD | No open (subject?) |
| Mainfreight | Logistics | Yes | Yes | Yes | Booked | Excellent response |

### Analyze & Iterate

**After each wave of sending, review:**
- Which subject line formulas work best per industry?
- Which industries respond fastest?
- Which email frames generate calls?
- Which decision-makers respond best?

**Update industry-frameworks with findings:**
- "Healthcare: CTA 'This week' works better than general timeframes"
- "Logistics: Lead with 'overtime' instead of 'staffing shortages' — higher open rate"
- "Manufacturing: Specific downtime $/hour metric drives more replies"
