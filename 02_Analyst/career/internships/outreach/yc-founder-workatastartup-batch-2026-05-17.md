## What the research says

### 1. Keep founder emails short

- Gong says executives spend very little time on cold email and that reply rates drop sharply once emails go past 100 words.
- Best range for founder-direct first touches: roughly 50-100 words.

### 2. Use short subjects anchored to their world

- Gong's guidance: short subject lines work best, especially when they look like an internal topic rather than a pitch.
- For this batch, use 2-4 word subjects tied to the company's operating problem, not "internship" or "opportunity."

### 3. Personalization has to be real

- Woodpecker's 20M-email analysis says advanced personalization materially improves reply rates.
- Do not use fake personalization, compliments, or generic "love what you're building" openers.
- Use one live signal from Work at a Startup / YC: hiring wedge, traction metric, or workflow bottleneck.

### 4. Resume beats one-pager in email 1 for this batch

- A one-pager is a tailored one-page project memo: problem, why it matters, what I would build in 6-8 weeks, what input I would need, and what success would look like.
- That can be powerful after interest, especially when there is no formal opening.
- But for this batch, the stronger first move is:
  - lead with who I am
  - show I read what they say they need
  - explain why my background fits that need
  - attach the resume so they do not have to infer my credibility
- In other words: the email does the targeting, the resume does the proof.

### 5. Follow up, but do not nag

- Woodpecker says the first follow-up increases reply rate meaningfully and that 2-3 follow-ups is the practical sweet spot.
- Use 2 follow-ups max for this batch unless a founder engages.

## Copy shift from the old template

### Old pattern

- Subject led with internship or engineering label.
- Body asked permission to send a one-page idea before leading with identity.
- Credibility line sometimes relied on older ClinicalHours phrasing that is not cleanly source-backed in current notes.

### New pattern

- Open with who I am: Shivam Kanodia, Texas A&M, Industrial and Systems Engineering
- Immediately tie the note to a need the company states online
- Use current proof points I can defend now:
  - ClinicalHours co-founder
  - 200+ users / early clinic traction
  - systems modeling / process analysis / data tooling
- Attach resume in email 1
- Ask about fit for the team / role rather than opening with a one-pager

## Follow-up templates

### Follow-up 1 - Day 4

```text

Wanted to close the loop here.

The reason I reached out is that [company-specific need] is the kind of systems problem I want to work on, and I think I could be useful there.

Happy to send anything else that would be helpful.

Shivam
```

```text

Shivam
```
