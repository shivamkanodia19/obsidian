# Dallas Mavericks Lightweight Outreach Strategy

## Goal

Do a **small, credible first wave now** for the `Research & Innovation Intern` role without overplaying your hand before you have Legends experience to reference.

This first wave is for:
- visibility
- fit signaling
- role insight

It is **not** for:
- asking for referrals immediately
- telling a long story
- messaging half the Mavericks org at once

## Best First-Wave Contacts

### 1. Lydia Mayte Chavez

- Current role: `Research & Innovation Intern`
- Why she is the best first contact:
  - same exact internship track
  - most approachable
  - publicly posted about returning to the role
  - explicitly thanked `Ronnie Fauss`, `Yannis Shafi`, `Gabrielle Colunga`, and `Anisha Patel`
- Public evidence:
  - Her LinkedIn post says she is returning as a `Research & Innovation Intern` and is looking forward to learning about `business development, market research, and innovation within the sports industry`.
  - Her public LinkedIn profile says she is a `Junior at Baylor University studying Finance and Marketing with a concentration in Sales`.
- Strategic takeaway:
  - She is the cleanest source for what the team actually values.
  - Because she already thanked the people involved in hiring, she also confirms who matters in this process.
- Best ask:
  - `What made someone successful in the role?`
- What to reference:
  - you applied
  - your background is strategy + analytics
  - you want to understand how the role is actually structured
- Do not:
  - ask her to forward your resume
  - dump credentials

### 2. Gabrielle Colunga

- Current role: `Talent Acquisition Manager`
- Why she is a top first-wave contact:
  - closest recruiting-side route to your application
  - publicly tied to Mavericks hiring
  - explicitly named by Lydia in the R&I intern thank-you post
- Public evidence:
  - Dallas Mavericks LinkedIn publicly welcomed `Gabrielle Colunga - Talent Acquisition Manager`.
  - Her public LinkedIn profile shows she is at the Dallas Mavericks and previously worked in recruiting roles including `Inflight Recruiter at Southwest Airlines`.
  - Her public LinkedIn content includes practical advice for students: proofread resumes, remove fluff, keep it relevant, keep it to one page, and focus on measurable results.
- Strategic takeaway:
  - She likely responds best to concise, polished, role-specific outreach.
  - If you message her, your note should read like someone who respects recruiting time and already understands how to present relevant experience.
- Best ask:
  - `I wanted to introduce myself directly and share why I believe the role is a strong fit.`
- What to reference:
  - market analysis
  - business modeling
  - executive-facing research
  - one sports-adjacent proof point, but without mentioning Legends by name yet if you want to stay conservative
- Do not:
  - ask "can you refer me"
  - send a wall of text
  - attach multiple files

### 3. Yannis Shafi, CPA

- Current role: `Business Development Manager`
- Why he is one of the best function-side contacts:
  - directly in the part of the organization this internship supports
  - publicly thanked by Lydia in her R&I intern post
  - publicly involved in Mavericks externship / student-facing learning events
- Public evidence:
  - His public LinkedIn profile says he is currently a `Business Development Manager for the Dallas Mavericks`.
  - His public profile also references `The Sports Analytics Club Program`.
  - A High Point / LinkedIn externship post specifically thanks `Yannis Shafi, CPA (Business Development Manager)` for sharing knowledge, experiences, and career guidance.
  - His UT Dallas Alpha Kappa Psi alumni spotlight shows he held `Vice President of Public Relations` and came from an accounting background before his sports-business path.
- Strategic takeaway:
  - He looks like someone who talks to students and can appreciate a thoughtful, role-fit message.
  - He is probably the best person to validate whether your background maps to the work.
- Best ask:
  - `How does your team think about strong intern candidates for this role?`
- What to reference:
  - market research
  - forecasting / scenario analysis
  - translating analysis into recommendations
  - interest in the business-development side of sports
- Do not:
  - pitch yourself like a salesperson
  - over-index on pure technical skills

### 4. Anisha Patel, MBA

- Current role: `Director of Programs`
- Why she is strong but better as an optional fourth contact:
  - likely very important to intern-program operations
  - repeatedly named in intern appreciation posts
  - likely sees a high volume of student outreach
- Public evidence:
  - The Org lists her as `Director Of Programs` for the Dallas Mavericks.
  - The Org also says she previously led high-performing MBA and internship programs at AT&T.
  - Logan Anderson's public post says the Mavericks' 8-week internship program was led by `Anisha Patel, MBA, Angela Nguyen, Anthony Randall, and Jennifer Fletcher`.
  - Lydia's R&I post also thanks Anisha directly.
- Strategic takeaway:
  - She is a real decision-adjacent person, but I would not contact her in the same first burst as Gabrielle unless one of the other messages goes unanswered.
  - Better to keep her as wave `1.5` or `2`.
- Best ask:
  - `What distinguishes the strongest interns in the Mavericks program?`

## Sequence Strategy

### Option A: Best practical sequence

Day 1:
- Send LinkedIn connection note to `Lydia`

Day 2:
- Send LinkedIn message or email to `Gabrielle`

Day 4 or 5:
- Send LinkedIn note to `Yannis`

Day 8 to 10:
- If no traction, send a short note to `Anisha`

### Why this works

- Each message has a different purpose.
- You avoid looking like you copied the same note to everyone.
- You preserve a stronger second wave for mid-summer when you have internship substance.

## Best Experience To Leverage Now

Use these now:

- `ClinicalHours`: founder execution, market analysis, GTM, unit economics
- `forecasting + scenario modeling`: shows structured business thinking
- `executive-facing recommendations`: strong for this role
- `sports-adjacent commercial analysis`: acceptable to mention at a high level even before you start the internship

Do not lean too hard on:

- technical stacks
- generic passion for sports
- "I want to break into sports"

## Best Proof Point Rotation

Use only one proof point per message.

### For Lydia

- `I come from a strategy + analytics background and recently worked on sports-adjacent commercial analysis, so the role stood out immediately.`

### For Gabrielle

- `My strongest work has been market analysis, business modeling, and turning research into executive-facing recommendations.`

### For Yannis

- `I'm most interested in the point where research and analysis become actual business decisions, which is why this role stood out to me.`

### For Anisha

- `I'm especially drawn to roles where the internship is clearly designed around real ownership and learning, not just support tasks.`

## Message Constraints

Keep first-touch outreach:

- under `75` words for LinkedIn connection notes
- under `110` words for email or post-connection notes
- one clear ask
- no attachments unless requested

## Current Recommendation

Do the first wave **now**, but keep it to `3` people:

1. `Lydia`
2. `Gabrielle`
3. `Yannis`

Hold `Anisha` unless:
- one of the first three responds and gives you a reason to contact her
