# Startup Founder and Hiring Outreach Batch - 2026-05-17

## Goal

Build a broader startup outreach batch that goes beyond YC while keeping the same bar:

- real company need stated online
- public and source-backed email route
- short first-touch note
- resume attached in email 1

## What changed

### 1. Lead with identity, not the ask

The first line should say who I am:

`I'm Shivam Kanodia, a Texas A&M student studying Industrial and Systems Engineering.`

That gives immediate context and makes the note feel grounded.

### 2. Resume is the best first move here

A one-pager is a tailored one-page memo on a company problem: what I think matters, what I would work on, and what success could look like.

That can help later.

For this batch, it is not the best first touch.

The stronger sequence is:

1. lead with who I am
2. point to the need they already say they have
3. explain why my background fits that need
4. attach the resume so they can evaluate fast

## Review findings

Three fresh-context reviewers converged on the same problems in the first pass:

- too much job-post mirroring
- too much preference language
- not enough concrete proof
- weak CTA
- the batch sounded templated too quickly

The rewrite rule for v2:

- identity in sentence one
- bottleneck in sentence two
- mechanism in sentence three
- small ask in sentence four

## First-touch rules

- Say `Industrial and Systems Engineering`, fully spelled out.
- Do not say `freshman`.
- Use one concrete company-specific need from their job page, YC page, or careers page.
- Use one concrete proof line from my background:
  - ClinicalHours co-founder
  - 200+ users / early operational traction
  - systems modeling / data analysis / process work at Texas A&M
- Keep the note concise and practical.
