# Email Verification Tools for Cold Outreach

Find and verify CEO/founder emails for 20+ company outreach in <2 hours.

---

## Tools Overview

### Hunter.io
**What it does:** Finds emails for any company + validates deliverability

**Pricing:** Free (limited), $99/mo (500 searches)

**Accuracy:** 85–95% for public figures, lower for masked personal emails

**Best for:** Quick CEO lookups, bulk domain research

**How to use:**
1. Enter domain (e.g., intellicentrics.com)
2. Browse company email directory
3. Look for CEO/founder in results
4. Cross-check with LinkedIn

**Limitation:** Masks some executive emails in "p***@domain.com" format (paid tier shows full)

---

### Tomba.io
**What it does:** Similar to Hunter but stronger on European companies + pattern detection

**Pricing:** Free tier (limited), $99/mo

**Accuracy:** 80–90%

**Best for:** Secondary verification when Hunter shows masked email

**How to use:**
1. Enter domain
2. View email pattern (e.g., firstname@domain.com)
3. Construct email for target person
4. Verify with LinkedIn

---

### RocketReach
**What it does:** Broad B2B database of executive contact info + phone numbers

**Pricing:** Free preview, credits-based (expensive for bulk)

**Accuracy:** 75–85%

**Best for:** Verified phone numbers + secondary email confirmation

**How to use:**
1. Search CEO name + company
2. Review multiple data sources
3. Grab phone number if available
4. Email + phone together = high confidence

---

### ZoomInfo
**What it does:** Largest B2B contact database in US

**Pricing:** Enterprise (expensive), trial available

**Accuracy:** 80–90%

**Best for:** Bulk lookups if you have corporate access

**How to use:**
1. Company domain search
2. Executive directory with verified contact info
3. Export + organize

---

### Apollo.io
**What it does:** All-in-one prospecting platform (emails + data + sequencing)

**Pricing:** Free tier (limited), $49/mo+ for outreach

**Accuracy:** 85–92% (integrates multiple sources)

**Best for:** If you're doing ongoing cold outreach (bundles email + follow-up tracking)

**How to use:**
1. Search company + role
2. Auto-enrich with email + phone
3. Export for campaigns
4. Track opens/clicks in platform

## Workflow for 20-Company Verification (Tested Apr 2026)

**Time: 1–2 hours total**

### Step 1: Bulk Lookup (30 min)
1. Make spreadsheet: Company | CEO Name | Website
2. Run each company through Hunter.io domain search
3. Grab CEO email if visible (or note "masked")
4. Copy results to spreadsheet

### Step 2: Cross-Check Masked Emails (20 min)
If email shows as "p***@miwd.com":
1. Check Hunter pattern (firstname@domain pattern)
2. LinkedIn: Search CEO name + company
3. Try to infer from company email pattern
4. Note as "masked, inferred" vs. "verified"

### Step 3: Phone + Secondary Verification (20 min)
1. RocketReach or ZoomInfo for phone numbers
2. LinkedIn company page for contact info
3. Company website "Contact Us" page often has generic email
4. Use generic as fallback if personal email not found

### Step 4: Final Spreadsheet

## Verification Confidence Levels

### ✅ High Confidence (Use Direct)
- Email from company domain
- Verified by 2+ sources (Hunter + LinkedIn + company site)
- Recent activity on LinkedIn (last 30 days)

Example: msheehan@intellicentrics.com (verified in Hunter, LinkedIn, company directory)

### 🟡 Medium Confidence (Try First)
- Email from company domain but generic (info@, contact@)
- Or personal email from 1 source only
- Will mention CEO name in subject/body

Example: info@miwindows.com — "For Pete DeSoto: Production downtime reduction"

### ❌ Low Confidence (Use Phone)
- Email not found anywhere
- Only generic company contact available
- Call main line to ask for CEO email

Example: HD Supply (no personal email) → Call (972) 874-6680 for facility manager

---

## Red Flags

❌ Email from free Gmail/Outlook account (likely wrong person)
❌ Email format inconsistent with company domain (e.g., "p.desoto@gmail.com" for CEO)
❌ LinkedIn shows person left the company 6+ months ago
❌ No activity on LinkedIn in 1+ year (might be inactive/retired)

---

## Tips for Best Results

1. **LinkedIn is your truth checker** — Always verify CEO current role on LinkedIn before sending
2. **Company websites are good for generic emails** — Use "Contact Us" page as fallback
3. **Phone > email for uncertain fits** — If you're unsure of email validity, call to confirm
4. **Group similar domains** — If searching similar companies, patterns emerge (e.g., all use firstname@domain)
5. **Save patterns** — Once you find one CEO email in an industry, others often follow same pattern
