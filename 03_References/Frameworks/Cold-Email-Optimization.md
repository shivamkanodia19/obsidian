# Cold Email Optimization Framework

Recipient-focused cold email that drives responses from busy executives who scan emails in <20 seconds.

---

## Core Principle

Executives ask in order:
1. **Who is this?** (From: name + subject)
2. **Why should I care in 10 seconds?** (First 1–2 lines)
3. **What do you want?** (CTA clarity)
4. **How easy is this?** (Friction level)

---

## Email Structure (Optimized)

```
SUBJECT: [Specific outcome] ([Bounded timeframe])

Hi [Name],

[1 sentence: Why they should care + specific outcome]

[2–3 sentences: HOW (brief) + Proof/Specificity]

[CTA: Specific ask + Timeframe + Easy next step]

[Signature: Just name + phone]
```

### Guidelines by Section

**Subject Line:**
- Formula: `[Specific % improvement] in [Timeframe]`
- Example: "Cut credentialing from 120 days → 40 (8-week pilot)"
- Goal: Open rate 20–30% (vs. 10–15% for generic subjects)
- Source: HubSpot cold email benchmarks

**First Line:**
- Must answer: "Why does this matter to THIS person's business?"
- Don't say: "I researched your company" (signals sales pitch, effort-focused)
- Say: "Your [specific problem] is [impact]" (problem-focused)
- Example: "Your credentialing cycle is revenue drag"
- Source: Copywriting principle (recipient focus)

**Body:**
- Length: 50–70 words total (not 120–150)
- Structure: Problem → Solution outcome → Proof/specificity
- Specificity rule: Every claim needs a number (80%, 40 days, 48 hours)
- Don't: Explain yourself, credentials, company mission
- Do: Show you understand their problem + offer clear outcome

**CTA:**
- Formula: `[Action] + [Specific timeframe] + [Easy next step]`
- Example: "Call Wednesday or Thursday—10-minute overview"
- Avoid: "Worth 30 min?" (weak, vague, makes recipient decide)
- Avoid: "Let me know if you're interested" (open-ended)
- Goal: Specific ask → 2–5x higher response rate
- Source: Sales psychology (commitment + specificity)

**Signature:**
- Minimal: Name + phone only
- Don't include LinkedIn/credentials (you haven't earned credibility yet)
- Add credentials AFTER they respond

---

## What NOT to Do

❌ Lead with "I researched..."  
❌ Self-focused content ("I'm a TAMU junior", "looking for internship")  
❌ Vague improvements ("~60%", "significantly reduce")  
❌ Weak CTAs ("Open to exploring?")  
❌ Too much explanation (they'll tune out)  
❌ No urgency ("whenever you're available")

## Subject Line Formulas (Tested)

Ranked by open rate (B2B cold email benchmarks):

1. **[Specific % improvement] in [Timeframe]** (20–30% open rate)
   - "Cut scheduling time by 80% (8 weeks)"
   - "Eliminate 40+ hours/week of manual work"

2. **[Question about their pain]** (18–25% open rate)
   - "How much team time does your credentialing cycle eat?"
   - "What's your real downtime cost?"

3. **[Name] — quick [specific] question** (15–22% open rate)
   - "Mike—quick credentialing question"
   - "Fran—RN scheduling automation idea"

Avoid:
- "Follow-up" (signals spam)
- "Opportunity" (vague, pitch-like)
- Generic subject (no personalization)

---

## Response Rate Expectations

**Industry benchmarks (B2B cold email):**
- Open rate: 20–30%
- Reply rate: 2–5% (5% is very good)
- Meeting rate: 10–20% of replies

**Your goal (realistic):**
- 20 emails sent
- 4–6 opens + replies
- 1–2 calls booked

---

## Personalization Levels (Effort vs. ROI)

| Level | Effort | Open Rate | Reply Rate | Use Case |
|-------|--------|-----------|-----------|----------|
| **Generic** | 5 min | 15% | <1% | Don't use |
| **Name-only** | 5 min | 18% | 1% | Volume (many targets) |
| **Specific problem** | 15 min | 25% | 2–3% | Medium priority targets |
| **Researched + custom CTA** | 30 min | 30% | 3–5% | Top 5 priority targets |

---

## Follow-Up Cadence (If No Response)

- **Day 3:** Re-send with minor subject line variation (if no open)
- **Day 7:** LinkedIn message (different angle, shorter)
- **Day 10:** Phone call (if number available)
