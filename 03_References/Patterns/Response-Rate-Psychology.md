# Response Rate Psychology

This note captures the practical behavior patterns behind whether a recipient opens, scans, trusts, and replies to a cold message.

## Core Principles

- People triage email fast and punish cognitive load.
- A recipient usually decides whether to keep reading before they fully understand the whole message.
- Clear relevance beats cleverness when the sender has no existing trust.

## Common Reply Friction

- The message feels mass-sent or generic.
- The ask is larger than the credibility earned in the email.
- The benefit is abstract instead of concrete.
- The reader cannot quickly tell why they specifically were contacted.

## What Tends To Help

- Specificity that signals the sender did real homework
- One concrete value claim instead of several vague promises
- A low-friction ask such as a short reply or brief call
- Language that sounds respectful, direct, and easy to scan
