# Exam 3 - Question Types by Topic

## Overview
Exam 3 for TAMU Physics (Mechanics) typically covers dynamics, work-energy, rotational motion, and oscillations. This guide categorizes question types by topic based on patterns from 2015-2019 exams.

---

## Topic 1: Circular Motion & Centripetal Force

### Question Type 1A: Basic Circular Motion Calculations
- **Pattern:** Given radius, velocity, find centripetal acceleration
- **Key Formula:** $a_c = \frac{v^2}{r}$
- **Common Variations:**
  - Object on horizontal circular path
  - Conical pendulum problems
  - Minimum speed at top of vertical circle
- **Typical Difficulty:** Easy-Medium
- **Time to Solve:** 3-4 minutes

### Question Type 1B: Banked Curves & Normal Force
- **Pattern:** Friction, normal force, banking angle involved
- **Key Skills:** Free body diagram, vector decomposition
- **Common Scenarios:**
  - Car on banked circular road
  - Find minimum/maximum speed
- **Typical Difficulty:** Medium
- **Time to Solve:** 5-7 minutes

### Question Type 1C: Vertical Circular Motion (Loop-the-Loop)
- **Pattern:** Object moving in vertical circle, find normal force or minimum speed
- **Critical Points:** Top of loop, bottom of loop, breaking contact
- **Key Insight:** At top, minimum speed when $N = 0$
- **Typical Difficulty:** Medium-Hard
- **Time to Solve:** 6-8 minutes

## Topic 2: Work & Kinetic Energy

### Question Type 2A: Work-Energy Theorem
- **Pattern:** Calculate work done by each force, relate to change in kinetic energy
- **Equation:** $W_{net} = \Delta KE = \frac{1}{2}m(v_f^2 - v_i^2)$
- **Common Scenarios:**
  - Motion along incline with friction
  - Pulling object with rope at angle
  - Spring compression/extension
- **Typical Difficulty:** Easy-Medium
- **Time to Solve:** 4-5 minutes

### Question Type 2B: Multiple Forces & Work
- **Pattern:** 3-4 forces acting, calculate net work
- **Key Skill:** Resolving forces parallel/perpendicular to motion
- **Typical Difficulty:** Medium
- **Time to Solve:** 6-7 minutes

### Question Type 2C: Work with Graphs
- **Pattern:** Force vs. displacement graph provided, find work (area under curve)
- **Variation:** Work is positive, negative, or zero depending on force direction
- **Typical Difficulty:** Medium
- **Time to Solve:** 4-6 minutes

---

## Topic 3: Power

### Question Type 3A: Average Power
- **Pattern:** Given work done and time interval, calculate power
- **Equation:** $P_{avg} = \frac{W}{\Delta t}$
- **Common Scenarios:**
  - Lifting object at constant speed
  - Car climbing hill
- **Typical Difficulty:** Easy
- **Time to Solve:** 2-3 minutes

### Question Type 3B: Instantaneous Power
- **Pattern:** Power as function of velocity, $P = \vec{F} \cdot \vec{v}$
- **Common Variations:**
  - Constant force with changing velocity
  - Variable force with variable velocity
- **Typical Difficulty:** Medium
- **Time to Solve:** 4-5 minutes

---

## Topic 4: Potential Energy & Conservation

### Question Type 4A: Gravitational Potential Energy
- **Pattern:** Height change, find potential energy change
- **Equation:** $\Delta PE = mg\Delta h$
- **Common Scenarios:**
  - Object on incline
  - Dropping from height
  - Pendulum problems
- **Typical Difficulty:** Easy-Medium
- **Time to Solve:** 3-4 minutes

### Question Type 4B: Elastic Potential Energy (Springs)
- **Pattern:** Spring compressed/stretched, find PE or force
- **Equation:** $PE_{spring} = \frac{1}{2}kx^2$
- **Common Variations:**
  - Spring force vs. compression graph
  - Object on spring oscillating
- **Typical Difficulty:** Medium
- **Time to Solve:** 4-6 minutes

### Question Type 4C: Energy Conservation (No Non-Conservative Forces)
- **Pattern:** Use $E_i = E_f$ to solve for unknown
- **Total Energy:** $E = KE + PE_{grav} + PE_{spring}$
- **Common Scenarios:**
  - Pendulum at different heights
  - Block sliding down frictionless incline
  - Spring-mass system
- **Typical Difficulty:** Medium-Hard
- **Time to Solve:** 6-8 minutes

### Question Type 4D: Energy with Friction (Non-Conservative)
- **Pattern:** Account for energy lost to friction: $W_{friction} = -\mu mg \cdot d$
- **Equation:** $E_i - |W_{friction}| = E_f$
- **Common Scenarios:**
  - Block sliding down incline with friction
  - Spring-mass system with friction
  - Collision with energy loss
- **Typical Difficulty:** Medium-Hard
- **Time to Solve:** 7-9 minutes

## Topic 5: Rotational Motion Basics

### Question Type 5A: Angular Kinematics
- **Pattern:** Given angular velocity or acceleration, find angular displacement
- **Equations:** 
  - $\theta = \omega_i t + \frac{1}{2}\alpha t^2$
  - $\omega_f = \omega_i + \alpha t$
  - $\omega_f^2 = \omega_i^2 + 2\alpha\theta$
- **Connection to Linear Motion:** $v = r\omega$, $a = r\alpha$
- **Typical Difficulty:** Easy-Medium
- **Time to Solve:** 4-5 minutes

### Question Type 5B: Moment of Inertia Calculations
- **Pattern:** Calculate or identify $I$ for standard shapes
- **Common Shapes:**
  - Solid cylinder: $I = \frac{1}{2}MR^2$
  - Solid sphere: $I = \frac{2}{5}MR^2$
  - Thin hoop: $I = MR^2$
  - Thin rod about center: $I = \frac{1}{12}ML^2$
- **Parallel Axis Theorem:** $I = I_{cm} + Md^2$
- **Typical Difficulty:** Easy-Medium
- **Time to Solve:** 3-5 minutes

### Question Type 5C: Rotational Dynamics (Torque)
- **Pattern:** Find torque, angular acceleration, or angular momentum
- **Equations:**
  - $\tau = I\alpha$ (rotational analog of $F = ma$)
  - $\tau = rF\sin\theta$
- **Common Scenarios:**
  - Wheel with applied force
  - Pulley system
  - Door hinges
- **Typical Difficulty:** Medium
- **Time to Solve:** 5-7 minutes

---

## Topic 6: Rotational Energy

### Question Type 6A: Rotational Kinetic Energy
- **Pattern:** Find rotational KE or total KE of rolling object
- **Equation:** $KE_{rot} = \frac{1}{2}I\omega^2$
- **Total KE (rolling):** $KE_{total} = \frac{1}{2}mv^2 + \frac{1}{2}I\omega^2$
- **Connection:** For rolling without slipping: $v = r\omega$
- **Typical Difficulty:** Easy-Medium
- **Time to Solve:** 4-5 minutes

### Question Type 6B: Rolling Motion (With and Without Slipping)
- **Pattern:** Rolling object down incline, find speed at bottom
- **No Slipping Condition:** $v = r\omega$
- **Energy Approach:** $mgh = \frac{1}{2}mv^2 + \frac{1}{2}I\omega^2$
- **Key Insight:** Fraction of energy in rotation depends on shape
- **Typical Difficulty:** Medium-Hard
- **Time to Solve:** 6-8 minutes

### Question Type 6C: Angular Momentum & Conservation
- **Pattern:** Isolated system, angular momentum conserved
- **Equation:** $L = I\omega$ (constant if $\tau_{ext} = 0$)
- **Common Scenarios:**
  - Spinning skater pulling arms in
  - Object lands on spinning wheel
  - Collision with angular momentum exchange
- **Typical Difficulty:** Medium-Hard
- **Time to Solve:** 7-9 minutes

---

## Topic 7: Oscillations (Simple Harmonic Motion)

### Question Type 7A: SHM Basic Parameters
- **Pattern:** Identify or calculate amplitude, frequency, period, phase
- **Key Equations:**
  - $T = \frac{1}{f} = 2\pi\sqrt{\frac{m}{k}}$ (mass-spring)
  - $x(t) = A\cos(\omega t + \phi)$
  - $\omega = 2\pi f = \sqrt{\frac{k}{m}}$
- **Typical Difficulty:** Easy-Medium
- **Time to Solve:** 3-4 minutes

### Question Type 7B: Energy in SHM
- **Pattern:** Calculate total mechanical energy, KE at position
- **Total Energy:** $E = \frac{1}{2}kA^2$ (constant)
- **At Position x:** 
  - $KE = \frac{1}{2}k(A^2 - x^2)$
  - $PE = \frac{1}{2}kx^2$
- **Typical Difficulty:** Medium
- **Time to Solve:** 4-6 minutes

### Question Type 7C: Pendulum Problems
- **Simple Pendulum:** $T = 2\pi\sqrt{\frac{L}{g}}$ (small angle approximation)
- **Physical Pendulum:** $T = 2\pi\sqrt{\frac{I}{mgd}}$
- **Common Variations:**
  - Find period given length or angle
  - Determine frequency of oscillation
  - Energy at different points
- **Typical Difficulty:** Medium
- **Time to Solve:** 5-7 minutes

### Question Type 7D: Damped & Driven Oscillations
- **Pattern:** External force or friction affects oscillation
- **Damping:** Amplitude decreases exponentially: $A(t) = A_0 e^{-t/\tau}$
- **Resonance:** Driven oscillation at natural frequency → large amplitude
- **Typical Difficulty:** Hard
- **Time to Solve:** 8-10 minutes (less common on Exam 3)

## Study Strategy by Question Type

### Fast Questions (3-4 min) - Prioritize First
- Basic circular motion (Type 1A)
- Average power (Type 3A)
- Gravitational PE (Type 4A)
- Angular kinematics (Type 5A)
- Moment of inertia standard shapes (Type 5B)
- SHM basic parameters (Type 7A)

### Medium Questions (5-7 min) - Bread & Butter
- Work-energy theorem (Type 2A)
- Banked curves (Type 1B)
- Power with velocity (Type 3B)
- Spring PE (Type 4B)
- Rotational dynamics (Type 5C)
- Rotational kinetic energy (Type 6A)
- SHM energy (Type 7B)
- Pendulum (Type 7C)

### Hard Questions (7-10 min) - Save for Last
- Vertical circular motion (Type 1C)
- Energy with friction (Type 4D)
- Rolling motion (Type 6B)
- Angular momentum conservation (Type 6C)
- Damped oscillations (Type 7D)

## Common Exam Patterns

**Total Questions:** Usually 10-12 problems

**Typical Distribution:**
- Circular Motion: 1-2 questions
- Work & Energy: 2-3 questions
- Rotational Motion: 2-3 questions
- Oscillations: 1-2 questions
- Multi-topic integration: 1-2 questions

**Mixed Topic Questions:**
- Spring-mass system with friction (combines 4D, 5B, 7A)
- Rolling wheel on incline (combines 5A, 6B, 4C)
- Pendulum with collision (combines 7C, momentum, energy)

---

## Practice Tips

1. **Identify the question type first** - Read the question and classify it from this guide
2. **Time yourself** - Use the "Time to Solve" estimates to pace your exam
3. **Draw diagrams** - Especially for circular motion, forces, and energy conservation
4. **Check units** - Common error source
5. **Use energy when possible** - Often simpler than force analysis
6. **Write down given info** - Reduces careless errors
7. **Show work** - Even if answer is wrong, partial credit is available

## Key Equations Reference

| Topic | Equation | Variables |
|-------|----------|-----------|
| Centripetal | $a_c = \frac{v^2}{r}$ | $a_c$ = centripetal accel, $v$ = velocity, $r$ = radius |
| Work | $W = F d \cos\theta$ | Force component × distance |
| Kinetic Energy | $KE = \frac{1}{2}mv^2$ | $m$ = mass, $v$ = velocity |
| Potential Energy | $PE = mgh$ | $h$ = height above reference |
| Spring PE | $PE_s = \frac{1}{2}kx^2$ | $k$ = spring constant, $x$ = displacement |
| Power | $P = \frac{W}{t}$ or $P = F \cdot v$ | Work per unit time |
| Torque | $\tau = rF\sin\theta$ | Distance × force component |
| Moment of Inertia | $I = \int r^2 dm$ | Rotational mass |
| Angular Momentum | $L = I\omega$ | $I$ = moment of inertia, $\omega$ = angular velocity |
| Rotational KE | $KE_{rot} = \frac{1}{2}I\omega^2$ | Rotational kinetic energy |
| SHM Period (spring) | $T = 2\pi\sqrt{\frac{m}{k}}$ | Mass-spring system |
| SHM Period (pendulum) | $T = 2\pi\sqrt{\frac{L}{g}}$ | Simple pendulum |
