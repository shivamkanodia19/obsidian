Downloaded files:
- `2007_exam.pdf` through `2019_exam.pdf`
- matching solution PDFs when available

## Notes

- All 13 mechanics final exam PDFs from 2007-2019 were reachable and downloaded successfully.
- The exam PDFs are text-based and extract cleanly.
- Most solution PDFs appear to be image-heavy, so the analysis below is based mainly on the exam PDFs. This was not a blocker.

## Structure Patterns

- Every exam is free-response only.
- Every exam says `No calculators are allowed`.
- Every exam emphasizes showing work, boxing final answers, and earning partial credit by naming the law used.
- 2007-2012 and 2018 mostly use 6 larger problems.
- 2013-2017 and 2019 mostly use 7 shorter problems.
- Total points are usually `105`; 2019 is `100`.
- Many exams from roughly 2012 onward end with the same formula/reference sheet.

## Recurring Patterns

- Momentum is everywhere.
  - Collisions, explosions, sticking problems, or jump-on/jump-off platform setups appear in every year.

- Rotation is just as common.
  - Massive pulleys, rolling bodies, merry-go-rounds, Lazy Susans, and torque/braking questions show up constantly.

- Springs and SHM are a favorite.
  - Many finals include at least one spring oscillator, nonlinear spring, pendulum small-angle approximation, or physical pendulum problem.

- Friction is rarely simple.
  - They like variable friction, slipping thresholds, friction between stacked blocks, and “find the maximum before slipping starts.”

- They often blend topics inside one problem.
  - Common combinations are collision + pendulum, explosion + oscillator, rotation + friction, or energy + variable-force analysis.

- They reward setup more than arithmetic.
  - Several problems explicitly say “write the system of equations” or “obtain the equation, do not solve it.”

- Free-body diagrams matter a lot.
  - Many problems explicitly say they will not be graded without FBDs or a clear coordinate system.

- Conservative-force reasoning appears often.
  - Repeated tasks include proving a force is conservative, finding a potential energy function, locating turning points, or recovering force from `U(x)` or `KE(x)`.

- Story wrappers change, but the mechanics repeat.
  - Bugs, mice, cockroaches, skiers, yo-yos, cannonballs, and cars are mostly skins over the same small set of physics tools.

## Best Study Targets

- Momentum conservation in 1D and 2D, including explosions and perfectly inelastic collisions
- Rotational dynamics with pulleys, torques, and angular momentum conservation
- Spring/oscillator motion, especially SHM setup and small-angle approximations
- Friction-heavy Newton's-law problems with stacked blocks or inclines
- Work-energy with non-constant forces or non-constant friction
- Central-force / gravitational potential problems
