# ClinicalHours Business Model Overview

## Core Problem Solved
**Disorganized Volunteer Tracking** → Centralized volunteer infrastructure
- Current state: Clinics manage volunteers via spreadsheets, Google Forms, flooded emails
- Solution: ClinicalHours platform with centralized tracking, filtering, communications, scheduling

## Revenue Model
- **Current Status:** Pre-revenue (no customers paying)
- **Pricing:** TBD — currently undefined
- **Business Type:** SaaS (B2B clinic management + B2C student discovery)

## Ideal Customer Profile
- **Primary:** Non-profit free clinics with 15+ active volunteers
- **Why:** Large volunteer bases create clear ROI on centralization
- **Pain Point Fit:** Moving from email/spreadsheets to organized system
- **Decision Maker:** Clinic director or volunteer coordinator

## Target Segments
1. **Primary Focus:** Free clinics (independent, non-profit)
2. **Secondary Exploration:** Hospital networks (Baylor Scott & White, St. Joseph Health, Ascension)
   - Note: Currently only advisor relationships, not customers

## Product-Market Fit Status
- **Pilot Live:** BCS Free Health Clinic (Bryan College Station, TX) — fully onboarded
- **User Base:** 200+ student users (zero paid marketing)
- **Clinic Partners:** 1 active customer
- **Market Signal:** Organic user growth via TikTok suggests strong student demand side

## Go-to-Market Strategy
1. **Student Acquisition:** Organic/TikTok (paid-for volunteer opportunities)
2. **Clinic Acquisition:** Direct email & phone outreach to DFW free clinics
3. **Network Effect:** Clinic referrals (once one clinic adopts, others follow)

## Key Unknowns (To Solve)
1. **Pricing Model:** Annual subscription price?
2. **Pricing Sensitivity:** What can free clinics afford?
3. **Feature Prioritization:** What features drive customer adoption?
4. **Competitive Differentiation:** What prevents clinics from building in-house?
5. **Expansion Path:** Hospital networks vs. pure free-clinic focus?
