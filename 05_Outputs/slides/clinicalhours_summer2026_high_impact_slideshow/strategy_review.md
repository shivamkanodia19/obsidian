# ClinicalHours Summer 2026 High-Impact Strategy Review

## Current system audit

The existing ClinicalHours summer slideshow already moved in the right direction:

- brighter palette
- calmer CTA
- real product proof
- less pitch-deck energy

But it still had three weaknesses that make it feel more like a designed explainer than a swipe-stopping social asset:

1. Too many supporting labels, chips, and mini-cards compete with the hero proof.
2. The middle slides do not lock onto one continuous example strongly enough.
3. The first frame is cleaner than older decks, but it still does not hit with enough visual dominance for a short-form feed.

## Research-backed improvement areas

### 1. Make the first frame do more work

- TikTok's Creative Codes guidance says the first `6` seconds are vital and recommends a clear `hook -> body -> close` structure.
- TikTok's May `2026` out-of-phone guidance says content should feel authentic, sound-off friendly, and short and impactful.

Implication:

- Slide `1` must state the pain fast and show real product proof immediately.

### 2. Cut words, not meaning

- Meta's Reels guidance emphasizes `9:16` creative, safe-zone placement, and clear creative elements.
- TikTok's carousel playbook recommends safe-zone discipline and high-resolution imagery.

Implication:

- The slides should not become vague just because the copy is shorter.
- Fewer words should be compensated for by clearer proof crops and stronger hierarchy.

### 3. Use one coherent example in the middle of the deck

The strongest past ClinicalHours student branch (`v7semesterstart`) improved because it stayed on one real example instead of jumping between unrelated screenshots.

Implication:

- Slide `3` and slide `4` should continue the same clinic example so the viewer understands the workflow faster.

### 4. Build for silent autoplay first

- TikTok's May `2026` guidance explicitly calls out sound-off understanding.
- Instagram Reels help says reels should support vertical formatting and at least `720` resolution, and Meta's Reels ads guidance favors safe-zone-first creative.

Implication:

- Every slide must work with no narration, no music, and no caption dependence.

### 5. Keep the CTA reversible

ClinicalHours is still a trust-building product for cold traffic.

Implication:

- `Browse first. Sign in later.` is stronger than a hard sign-up push for awareness content.

## Plugin recommendations

These are the most useful plugins for improving this process in the current Codex environment:

### 1. `Figma`

Best use:

- editable Figma Slides versions
- faster visual exploration
- alternate deck concepts without rewriting the asset system

Why it matters:

- it is the cleanest path for higher-end layout polish and collaboration once a truthful proof sequence is defined

### 2. `Browser`

Best use:

- public-site screenshot refreshes
- QA on live visual proof before reusing older exports

Why it matters:

- it reduces the risk of stale or mismatched proof in future slide iterations

### 3. `Chrome`

Best use:

- authenticated capture refreshes for student or admin states
- repeatable logged-in proof gathering

Why it matters:

- the strongest student slides often depend on authenticated preview states

### 4. `Manus`

Best use:

- fast A/B concept generation
- alternate outlines for testing different hooks or pacing

Why it matters:

- it can generate additional polished deck directions quickly once the lane and constraints are clear

## Practical publishing recommendations

- Post this as a fast `9:16` slideshow or Reel, not as a static informational deck.
- Keep total runtime around `10-15` seconds if assembled into video.
- Add light motion in the posting tool: subtle zooms, not heavy animation.
- Keep the first frame readable before audio starts.
- For paid testing, create a `3`-slide cutdown and a `7`-slide expanded variant.
