# ClinicalHours Marketing Slideshow Narrative Plan

## Audience

Cold student-facing social traffic evaluating whether ClinicalHours makes the search for clinical opportunities feel easier and more actionable.

## Objective

Rebuild the canonical bright ClinicalHours marketing slideshow as an editable PowerPoint deck that preserves the current swipe logic, proof hierarchy, and calmer CTA strategy.

## Narrative Arc

1. Friction: finding clinical hours still feels scattered.
2. Discovery: the map creates immediate scale and payoff.
3. Simplification: preview-style clinic proof makes the product feel useful fast.
4. Direction: discovery should connect to tracking and logging in one account.
5. Activation: browsing first is a better close than forcing sign-in.

## Visual System

- Portrait social-slide canvas to match the existing ClinicalHours slideshow format.
- Bright editorial background with soft blue, mint, peach, and sand accents.
- Large upper-left headline block with one supporting sentence.
- Real ClinicalHours screenshots as the hero proof on every slide.
- Rounded white framing cards, dark ink typography, and small colored chips for rhythm.

## Editability Plan

- All headlines, subtext, section labels, CTA labels, and callout cards remain editable PowerPoint text.
- Product proof remains raster screenshots from the saved ClinicalHours captures.
- No slide relies on a flattened full-slide export for primary copy.
