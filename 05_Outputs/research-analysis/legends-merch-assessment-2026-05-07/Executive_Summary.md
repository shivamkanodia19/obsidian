# Legends Merchandise Sales Assessment

## Executive Summary
2024 in-stadium merchandise revenue increased modestly to **$2.774M**, up **0.5%** from **$2.761M** in 2023, with both seasons spanning **81 home games**. The growth story was driven more by **slightly higher transaction volume (+1.1%)** than by stronger baskets, as **sales per transaction declined 0.7%**. In other words, retail operations brought in slightly more buyers, but each buyer spent a bit less on average.

Category performance was led by **T-shirts (+$11.4K, +3.3%)**, followed by **Jerseys (+$6.0K, +0.6%)** and **Mugs (+$4.3K, +2.4%)**. Jerseys remained the core revenue engine at **37.8% of 2024 sales**, while Hoodies contributed another **23.8%**, showing continued reliance on premium apparel. On the downside, **Caps (-$5.5K, -1.8%)** and **Hoodies (-$3.3K, -0.5%)** softened year over year.

At the stadium-location level, **Lower Concourse** was the clearest bright spot, contributing **+$21.8K** in revenue growth, while **Main Concourse** added another **+$7.4K**. The main drags were **Club Level (-$7.5K)**, **Outfield (-$4.4K)**, and **Upper Deck (-$4.0K)**. The single biggest positive pocket was **Lower Concourse Jerseys (+$24.4K)**, while the largest decline came from **Outfield Jerseys (-$8.8K)**.

## Key Findings
- Revenue growth was positive but narrow: **+0.5% YoY** with no schedule-volume advantage.
- Traffic improved faster than basket size: **transactions +1.1%**, but **sales per transaction -0.7%**.
- **T-shirts** were the strongest material growth category; **Jerseys** remained the dominant revenue anchor.
- **Lower Concourse** captured the biggest gain and share increase, making it the best proof point for successful merchandising.
- **Club Level, Outfield, and Upper Deck** underperformed and need location-specific assortment or merchandising changes.
- A clear seasonal softness appeared in **July and August**, before rebounding in **September**.

## SWOT
### Strengths
- Revenue still grew despite already high scale and a flat number of home games.
- Jerseys and T-shirts showed durable fan demand.
- Lower Concourse produced strong, concentrated growth and offers a replicable template.

### Weaknesses
- Basket size declined, signaling weaker cross-sell or add-on performance.
- Three of five stadium zones declined year over year.
- Category growth was narrow, with too much reliance on a few premium product lines.

### Opportunities
- Expand **jersey and T-shirt adjacencies** in Lower and Main Concourse where demand is strongest.
- Launch **bundle and add-on programs** in July/August to lift units per transaction.
- Rework **Club Level, Outfield, and Upper Deck** assortments to reflect zone-specific demand patterns.
- Improve master data hygiene so future analysis can evaluate store-level tests more precisely.

### Threats
- Revenue concentration in **Jerseys + Hoodies** creates mix risk if premium demand softens.
- Flat overall growth may underwhelm relative to recent investment in retail operations.
- Inconsistent source data could reduce confidence in future store-level decisions if not corrected.

## Recommendations
1. Reallocate space and inventory toward **Jerseys and T-shirts** in Lower and Main Concourse, especially during peak traffic windows.
2. Recover basket size with **bundle offers** such as tee + keychain, cap + foam finger, or premium-apparel add-on prompts at checkout.
3. Use **zone-specific resets** for the weakest areas:
   - Club Level: address Cap and Jersey softness.
   - Outfield: rebuild Jersey performance.
   - Upper Deck: offset Jersey decline with sharper value-oriented assortment planning.
4. Build a **midseason promo calendar** for July/August, when both seasons showed the clearest softness.
5. Standardize data fields before next season:
   - Exclude malformed season labels.
   - Standardize item names (`Jersy` -> `Jersey`, trim whitespace).
   - Treat `store_zone` as the reliable location field unless `store_id` is fixed operationally.
