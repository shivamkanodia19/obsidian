# ClinicalHours TikTok Student Visual Audit And Variation Rules - 2026-06-02

## Why This Exists

The current student deck system finally has a branch that feels designed, persuasive, and logically sequenced without losing proof discipline.

That improvement should not turn into a new rigid template.

Future agents should preserve the standards that made the recent work stronger, while still changing the shell enough that a new deck does not feel like the same slideshow with swapped copy.

## Best Current Student Reference

`v7semesterstart` is the strongest current student-facing reference when the goal is:

- strong hook-to-CTA logic
- real screenshot proof that reads quickly
- visible variation from slide to slide
- a deck that feels designed instead of assembled

Use it as a quality benchmark, not as the next mandatory shell.

### Why `v7semesterstart` works

- Slide `1` works because the headline is fast, the supporting cards are short, and the proof crop is anchored to a recognizable search surface instead of a generic promo block.
- Slide `2` works because it breaks the shell on purpose. The deck shifts into a dark, map-led frame, so the second swipe feels like a new moment instead of a copy of slide `1`.
- Slide `3` works because one coherent screenshot proves the exact claim. The viewer can see the query, clinic name, city, and `More Info` path together.
- Slide `4` works because it continues the same Houston Methodist proof chain instead of introducing a different generic account surface.
- Slide `5` works because the CTA comes after the product has earned it. It reads as permission to start, not pressure to sign in.

## Good Patterns To Keep

- Keep one dominant proof asset per slide. Support crops are fine only when they sharpen the same action instead of introducing a new story.
- Keep the five-slide student sequence disciplined: hook, discovery, proof check, follow-through, low-friction CTA.
- Keep the proof chain continuous when a later slide depends on the earlier example. `v7semesterstart` slides `3` and `4` are the clearest model here.
- Keep visual variety inside the deck. A student deck gets stronger when one slide is map-led, one is proof-led, one is utility-led, and one is CTA-led.
- Keep the CTA calm. The strongest recent close is still `browse first` or `start as guest`, not a hard sign-in push.
- Keep note cards and secondary captions short. They should add direction, not become a second paragraph.

## Failure Patterns To Treat As Real Problems

### 1. Bottom keyword pills and taxonomy chips

Repeated bottom keyword chips were a major source of sameness in older student branches.

The pattern shows up heavily in `v3bright7`, `v5firstpath`, `v6realleads`, and `v6guestfirst`.

Problem:

- they make multiple branches feel like the same shell
- they often restate the slide instead of proving anything
- they pull attention away from the actual screenshot

Rule:

- treat `chip-stack` and `chip` as legacy accent patterns
- do not reintroduce them by default
- only use them if the labels are themselves meaningful proof, not just decorative summary words

### 2. Meta strategy labels showing up inside the creative

Older branches exposed internal framing language directly on the slide:

- `WHY IT LANDS`
- `WHY IT EXISTS`
- `AFTER THE PREVIEW`
- `AFTER THE FIRST SHIFT`

Problem:

- they sound like builder notes instead of audience copy
- they make the deck feel explained rather than persuasive
- they leak process language into the final creative

Rule:

- visible overlay text should sound like student-facing guidance or payoff, not commentary on why the slide works

### 3. Under-cropped or generic screenshot bands

Several older student slides are truthful but visually weak because the claimed action is too small inside a wide strip of dark UI.

Strong caution examples:

- `v6guestfirst` slide `4`
- `v5firstpath` slide `4`
- `v6realleads` slide `4`

Problem:

- the useful control is readable only after work
- the viewer sees empty dashboard space before they see the proof
- the deck starts to look like stacked website slices instead of designed proof

Rule:

- if the control or label supporting the claim is not one of the first readable things on the slide, the crop is not ready

### 4. Text-heavy story proof

`v6realleads` slide `4` is the clearest warning case.

Problem:

- the slide asks the viewer to read paragraphs to understand credibility
- the paragraph proof is real, but it is too slow for a swipe deck
- the supporting auth and dashboard panels do not rescue the claim because they are more generic than the topic

Rule:

- if the slide needs a paragraph to justify the claim, the proof is probably too slow or too indirect

### 5. Generic proof outranking specific proof

`v3bright7` slide `1` is polished, but the tall phone map only indirectly proves `Finding clinical hours still feels random.`

Problem:

- the screenshot is truthful
- the screenshot is visually attractive
- but the biggest visible idea is `map with opportunities`, not `search feels random`

Rule:

- do not confuse polished proof with specific proof
- the hero asset must prove the exact slide job, not just the general product category

### 6. Illogical sequence reversals

Older student branches sometimes drifted into a sequence where the deck:

- previews discovery
- jumps into authenticated or first-log proof
- then returns to a `browse first` close that feels like backtracking

Problem:

- the viewer is asked to move forward, then backward
- the CTA loses authority because the middle slides already implied a later stage

Rule:

- if the close is `browse first`, the middle of the deck cannot over-commit to a later-stage workflow
- the final CTA should feel like the natural next step, not a reset

### 7. Branch-to-branch shell cloning

`v3bright7` established a polished student shell, but later branches reused too much of its exact rhythm:

- similar cream gradient
- similar top-left headline block
- similar bottom chips
- similar stacked card treatment
- similar progress chrome with only small changes

Problem:

- new branches can look visually finished while still feeling repetitive
- the topic changes, but the emotional pacing does not

Rule:

- future branches should copy the discipline, not the exact shell

## Keep, Revise, Retire

- `v7semesterstart` - keep as the current best reference for varied slide roles, coherent proof chaining, and a stronger CTA payoff
- `v5firstpath` - keep as a sequence reference because its problem-to-action arc is strong, but revise visually if it is reused again
- `v3bright7` - revise before reuse because it is polished but too easy to clone and too broad for many issue-specific asks
- `v6guestfirst` - revise before reuse because the topic is good, but the guest-first proof still gets visually outranked by generic auth surfaces
- `v6realleads` - retire unless a future agent acquires much tighter proof; its trust claim currently outruns the visible evidence too often

## Variety Contract For Future Agents

The goal is similar quality, not similar decoration.

For any new durable student branch:

- keep the `hook -> discovery -> proof -> follow-through -> CTA` logic unless the topic clearly demands another sequence
- vary at least `3` of these `7` design dimensions from the nearest student branch

Choose from:

- palette family
- hero proof framing
- support-card geometry
- background structure
- overlay density
- CTA module shape
- whether the slide is copy-led or proof-led

Within one deck:

- at least one slide should be proof-first
- at least one slide should be copy-first
- at least one slide should use a darker or denser scene if the rest of the deck is light
- at least one slide should make the product control itself the main visual event

Do not vary randomly.

Preserve these anchors:

- clear ClinicalHours branding
- fast headline hierarchy
- one dominant proof moment per slide
- calm, credible student tone

## Crop And Readability Gate

Reject the slide if any of these are true:

- the headline says `browse as guest` but `Browse as guest` is not clearly visible in the proof crop
- the headline says `save` but `Save to Dashboard`, `Details`, or `Find App Link` is too small to read
- the headline says `check one result` but the clinic name, city, or next-step control cannot be read together
- the viewer sees large dark dead space before the useful control
- the screenshot asks the viewer to read a paragraph to understand the slide
- the slide uses more than one secondary crop that is not part of the same proof chain

## Durable Principle

Protect the standard, not the sameness.

The right future outcome is not `make more decks that look like v7semesterstart`.

The right future outcome is:

- keep `v7semesterstart`-level proof discipline
- keep `v7semesterstart`-level sequencing clarity
- keep `v7semesterstart`-level visual confidence
- but let each new branch earn a different shell when the topic changes

## Cross-Lane Carryover

These rules also explain why `v8conciseops` should not become the concise default outside the student lane.

- Shorter copy is not enough if the slides still share the same shell and proof rhythm.
- A concise deck still fails if the screenshot text is too wordy or too slow to parse.
- Future concise clinic-ops work should borrow `v7semesterstart`-level variety and proof readability, then shorten the copy further.
