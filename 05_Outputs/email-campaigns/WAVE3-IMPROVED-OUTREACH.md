# Wave 3 Internship Outreach — IMPROVED VERSION

**What changed (Apr 15):** Reframed all emails from vendor pitches to internship inquiries. Updated contact routing. Fixed email tone + CTA approach.

**Why changed:** Current emails were too direct/confident, sounding like consultant pitches instead of internship exploration. This weakens response rates from busy executives.

---

## CRITICAL IMPROVEMENTS MADE

### Email Framing (Strongest Fix)
- ❌ **Before:** "I can automate your 120-day cycle" (assertion)
- ✅ **After:** "I'm exploring this as an internship project; would you be open to discussing how you approach it?" (curiosity)

**Impact:** Removes pressure, adds authentic learning intent, more likely to get response from busy decision-makers.

### CTAs (Less Aggressive)
- ❌ **Before:** "Call Wednesday or Thursday this week" (demanding)
- ✅ **After:** "Would you be open to a 10-minute call?" (inviting)

**Impact:** Reduces friction, especially for cold outreach from someone seeking internship.

### Contact Routing (Strategic)
- ❌ **Before:** All emails sent same way (direct to email address)
- ✅ **After:** Wave 3 uses three routes:
  - **Direct email** (Mike Sheehan, Dr. Rapp, James Childers) ← Ready to send immediately
  - **Phone first** (Fran Kirley) → Call (972) 724-0996 to get direct contact
  - **LinkedIn first** (Pete/Matt DeSoto) → Find direct contact before sending email

**Impact:** Better success rate by using right channel for each contact type.

## FAQs

**Q: Why not just send all 5 at once?**  
A: Tier 1b needs routing first (Fran's phone call, Pete's LinkedIn). Plus, Wave 3 is 5 targets—test the approach before scaling to 50–70 in Waves 4–5.

**Q: Should I mention TAMU or my class year?**  
A: Not in initial email. Let the conversation develop first; then add credentials if they ask or after they respond.

**Q: What if they don't respond to the email?**  
A: Day 7 → LinkedIn message (different angle). Day 10 → Phone call. Day 14 → Mark no-response.

**Q: What if they say no?**  
A: Perfectly fine. Log it, ask why if comfortable, and move to Wave 4 targets. Not every company will want to explore.

**Q: What if they ask what you want?**  
A: "I'm exploring an internship this summer in AI operations / data analytics. Your [problem] is exactly what I want to dig into. If you see potential, I'd love to discuss it further."
