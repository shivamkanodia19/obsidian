# LinkedIn Outreach to Company Leaders — Research-Backed Framework

## Core Principles

Based on 2026 LinkedIn research:
- **Personalization beyond first name** = +340% reply rate
- **Sequenced follow-ups** (2-5 day gaps) = +49% conversion lift
- **Specific mentions** (not generic) = 2x better response
- **Short, natural messages** beat corporate templates

---

## Message 1: Connection Request (Same Day)

**When:** Immediately after identifying leader
**Character limit:** 300 max

### Template

```
[Name], I've been following [Company]'s work on [specific thing: X initiative/product/market position] and your perspective on [specific achievement] stood out. 

I'm [your name], graduating ISE from TAMU in May 2026—working on [your focus: supply chain optimization/logistics/etc] and think your approach to [specific thing] is exactly the kind of problem-solving I'm trying to learn.

Would love to connect and hear what you're working on right now.
```

### Personalization Hooks

- **What research tells them you did:** Mention 1 specific thing about company/person (not "I like your company")
  - Recent news item
  - Product they built
  - Problem they're solving
  - Industry position
  - LinkedIn post they made

- **Mutual interest angle:** What you're learning that relates to their work
  - Not: "I want to work at your company"
  - Yes: "Your approach to X is exactly the problem I'm interested in"

### Examples (By Industry)

**Logistics/Supply Chain Company:**
```
[Name], I've been following [Company]'s work on predictive demand planning. That white paper on reducing bullwhip effect caught my attention.

I'm [your name], ISE senior at TAMU, working on supply chain optimization. The way you're approaching forecast accuracy is what I want to understand better.

Would love to connect and learn what you're seeing in the market right now.
```

**Healthcare/Operations:**
```
[Name], saw [Company]'s initiative on clinic operations efficiency. That's exactly the kind of process optimization I'm interested in.

I'm [your name], graduating ISE from TAMU in May. Working on how to reduce operational friction in healthcare specifically.

Would appreciate the chance to connect and hear what you're focusing on.
```

**Manufacturing/Operations:**
```
[Name], been following [Company]'s push on lean operations. Your recent [specific initiative] is interesting.

I'm [your name], ISE at TAMU. My focus is manufacturing systems optimization. Your team's approach aligns with what I'm trying to learn.

Curious to connect and understand what's working for you right now.
```

## Message 2: Post-Connection Message (Day 3)

**When:** 3 days after they accept connection
**Timing:** Tuesday-Thursday, 9am-12pm their time zone

### Template (Short & Genuine)

```
Hey [Name],

Quick note—I've been deep in [Company]'s approach to [specific problem] and it connects to a project I'm working on right now.

Do you have 15 minutes in the next few weeks to chat about how you're thinking about [specific challenge/opportunity]? Would love to get your perspective.

No pressure if timing isn't right.

—[Your name]
```

### Key Rules

- **Short paragraph breaks** (looks like text, not email)
- **One specific ask** (not "let's grab coffee," not "tell me about your company")
- **Permission to say no** (removes pressure)
- **Why now** (tie to what they're doing, not what you want)
- **Specific time frame** (not "sometime")

### Examples

**To Logistics Director:**
```
Hey [Name],

Been looking at how you're handling seasonal demand swings at [Company]. That's the exact optimization problem I'm trying to understand better.

Do you have 15 minutes in the next few weeks? Curious how you're thinking about forecast accuracy during peak season.

No pressure at all if timing's off.

—[Your name]
```

**To Operations VP:**
```
Hey [Name],

Saw your note about [recent initiative/challenge]. That's what I'm actually working on right now in a research context.

Curious if you have 15 minutes to chat about what's been working for you? Would help me understand the real constraints you're hitting.

Totally fine if this is bad timing.

—[Your name]
```

## Message 3: Follow-Up (Day 7 if no response)

**When:** 7 days after Message 2
**Intent:** Soft reminder, add value

### Template

```
[Name], just wanted to follow up on my last message.

Saw [recent news about company/initiative]. Made me think about [connection to your work].

Anyway, if you do find 15 minutes in the next few weeks, I'd value your take on [specific thing]. If not, totally understand.

—[Your name]
```

### Key Rules

- **Add something new** (recent news, new angle, different context)
- **Not "did you see my email?"** (shows you respect their inbox)
- **Reinforces one specific ask** (not multiple asks)
- **Low pressure** (they can ignore)

---

## Message 4: Final Follow-Up (Day 12 if still no response)

**When:** 12 days after Message 2
**Intent:** Graceful exit, door stays open

### Template

```
[Name], I'll let you get back to it.

Appreciate you taking the time to connect. If your schedule opens up down the road and you're interested in a quick chat, send me a note.

Good luck with [specific initiative/challenge they're facing].

—[Your name]
```

### Key Rules

- **No ask** (you're removing pressure)
- **Acknowledges their reality** (they're busy)
- **Door stays open** (genuine, not sarcastic)
- **Shows you paid attention** (mention their work)
- **This is the last touch** (then move on)

## Personalization Requirements (Critical)

For each leader, you need:

1. **One specific thing about them/company**
   - Recent news/initiative
   - Product/service they built
   - Market position or problem they're solving
   - Article they wrote or post they made
   - Stat about their company

2. **Connection to your work**
   - How their problem relates to what you study
   - Why their approach matters to your learning
   - What you're trying to understand about their space

3. **Genuine curiosity hook**
   - Not "I want to work there"
   - Not "can you help me get a job"
   - Yes: "I want to understand how you think about X"

## Tone Guide: How to Sound Like a Real Human

### ✅ DO

- Use short lines and natural breaks
- Mention ONE specific thing about them
- Ask for their perspective (not help)
- "Curious to hear..." not "I wanted to ask you about..."
- Conversational: "Been following your work on..." not "I have been observing..."
- Real: "No pressure at all" not "I understand you're busy"

### ❌ DON'T

- "I am reaching out to discuss synergies"
- "I would like to inquire about opportunities"
- Generic: "I'm impressed by your company"
- Multiple asks in one message
- All caps for emphasis
- Exclamation points at the end of every sentence
- "Excited to connect with you"
- Sales speak: "I'd love to share how I can add value"
